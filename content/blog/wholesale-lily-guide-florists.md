# Wholesale Lilies for Florists: Varieties, Pricing, and Handling Guide

Lilies are one of the most dramatic flowers in a florist's arsenal. A single stem can have 3-5 blooms, fills a room with fragrance, and commands premium retail pricing. They're the #1 flower for sympathy work, a staple in luxury arrangements, and a reliable year-round seller.

But lilies also come with unique challenges: pollen that stains everything, fragrance that some people find overwhelming, and buds that may or may not open. This guide covers what florists need to know to source, handle, and sell lilies profitably.

## Pollen Management

This is the #1 operational issue with lilies. Lily pollen permanently stains clothing, tablecloths, and skin.

### Prevention

1. **Remove anthers before they open.** When the bud starts opening but pollen hasn't yet matured (still firm and light-colored), gently pull or snip the anthers off
2. **Use tissue or gloves.** Never touch mature pollen with bare hands
3. **Brief clients.** Include a note: "Remove pollen anthers as blooms open to prevent staining"

### If Pollen Gets on Fabric

- **Don't rub or use water** — this sets the stain
- **Use tape.** Press sticky tape against the pollen and lift it off
- **Sunlight.** Place the fabric in direct sunlight for a few hours — UV breaks down lily pollen pigment
- **Commercial stain remover** after the bulk is removed with tape

### Pollen-Free Options

- **Asiatic lilies:** Naturally have less problematic pollen
- **Double Oriental lilies (Roselily):** Bred pollen-free, no stamen. Premium price ($4-6/stem) but zero pollen risk
- **Calla lilies:** Minimal pollen, contained within the spathe

## Care and Handling

### At Receiving

1. **Unpack immediately** — lilies are sensitive to ethylene in closed boxes
2. **Cut stems** 2 inches at 45-degree angle
3. **Remove lower leaves** — only keep leaves in the top 1/3 of the stem
4. **Place in clean water** with flower food at room temperature
5. **Store at 36-40°F** (2-4°C). Cooler temperature slows bud opening
6. **Remove anthers** as blooms begin to open (before pollen matures)

### Bud Opening Strategy

Lilies are typically shipped with buds in various stages. You can control the opening:

- **Speed up:** Place in warm water (75-80°F), in a warm room with bright light
- **Slow down:** Keep in cooler at 34-36°F. Tight buds can hold for 5-7 days in cold storage
- **For events:** Order with buds 3-4 days before the event. They'll open perfectly on time with room temperature water

## Design Applications

### Sympathy
Lilies are the #1 sympathy flower. White Orientals (Casa Blanca, Siberia) are the standard:
- **Standing spray:** 8-12 Oriental lily stems as focal + white roses + greenery
- **Casket spray:** Oriental lilies + roses + hydrangeas
- **Sympathy vase:** 5 Oriental + eucalyptus = elegant, fragrant, long-lasting

### Wedding
- **Bridal bouquet:** Calla lilies = the modern classic (12-15 stems, clean lines)
- **Centerpiece:** 3-4 LA hybrid stems + roses + greenery = luxurious
- **Ceremony:** Oriental lilies for fragrance in the ceremony space
- **Budget tip:** Asiatic lilies give you the lily look at 50% of the Oriental cost

### Luxury Retail
- **Single-variety:** 5-7 Oriental stems (Casa Blanca) in a tall vase = $85-120 retail showstopper
- **Mixed luxury:** Oriental lilies + roses + ranunculus = the triple-threat premium arrangement
- **Corporate:** Asiatic or LA hybrid lilies for weekly accounts (no fragrance complaints, long life)

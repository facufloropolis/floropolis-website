# How to Start a Dried Flower Business: Complete Guide for 2026

Dried flowers are one of the fastest-growing segments in the floral industry. They don't wilt, they don't need water, they ship easily, and they appeal to the sustainability-conscious consumer who wants flowers that last months (or years) instead of weeks.

For florists and entrepreneurs, dried flowers offer something fresh flowers can't: shelf-stable inventory with zero waste and flexible production timelines.

## Why Dried Flowers Are Booming

- **Zero waste.** No flowers thrown out at end of week
- **No cold storage needed.** Store at room temperature
- **Ship anywhere.** No cold chain required, lower shipping costs
- **Long shelf life.** Products last 1-3 years
- **Higher margins.** Buy wholesale fresh, dry yourself, sell at premium dried prices
- **Growing demand.** Dried flower market growing 12-15% annually
- **Sustainability angle.** Appeals to eco-conscious consumers

### Flowers to Avoid (Don't Dry Well)

- **Tulips** — Shrivel and lose shape
- **Hydrangeas** (fresh) — Collapse unless dried at perfect timing
- **Lilies** — Petals become papery and lose all visual appeal
- **Gerbera daisies** — Curl and discolor
- **Orchids** — Shrivel completely

## Drying Methods

### Air Drying (Simplest, Best for Beginners)

1. Strip lower leaves
2. Bundle 5-8 stems with a rubber band
3. Hang upside down in a dark, dry, warm space (closet, attic, garage)
4. Wait 1-3 weeks until stems snap (not bend)
5. Remove and store/arrange

**Key factors:** Darkness preserves color. Warmth speeds drying. Low humidity prevents mold.

### Silica Gel Drying (Best for Color Preservation)

1. Place 1-2 inches of silica gel in an airtight container
2. Place flower face-up on the gel
3. Gently pour more silica around and over the flower
4. Seal the container
5. Wait 3-7 days
6. Remove flower carefully, brush off excess gel

**Best for:** Roses, peonies, dahlias — flowers where you want to preserve shape and color perfectly.

### Pressing (Best for Flat Flowers)

1. Place flowers between parchment paper
2. Press inside heavy books or a flower press
3. Wait 2-4 weeks
4. Remove carefully

**Best for:** Pansies, daisies, ferns, small flowers. Used in frames, cards, and resin art.

### Pricing Strategy

- **Cost of fresh flowers × 3-5 = retail dried price.** The drying process, labor, and zero-waste premium justify the markup
- **Compare to competitors** on Etsy. Search your product type and price in the middle of the range
- **Bundle for value.** A "dried bouquet + vase" bundle at $65 sells better than a $50 bouquet alone

## Selling Dried Flowers

### Etsy (Best Starting Platform)

- **Market size:** "Dried flowers" gets 200K+ monthly searches on Etsy
- **Listing cost:** $0.20 per listing + 6.5% transaction fee
- **Photography is everything.** Styled, well-lit photos on Etsy = sales
- **Keywords matter.** "Dried flower bouquet," "boho dried flowers," "dried wedding flowers"
- **Shipping advantage.** Dried flowers ship flat or in a regular box. No cold chain

### Instagram + Website

- **Instagram:** Post styled photos of your dried arrangements. Use hashtags: #driedflowers #driedflowerbouquet #driedflowerarrangement
- **Website:** Squarespace or Shopify. Direct sales = higher margin than Etsy (no platform fees)
- **Local delivery:** Offer local pickup/delivery for dried arrangements

### Farmers Markets and Craft Fairs

- **Display advantage:** Dried flowers don't wilt in the sun or need water at a booth
- **Price range:** $15-75 per item at markets
- **Reusable inventory:** Unsold dried flowers come back next week (unlike fresh)

# Wholesale Hydrangeas for Florists: Sourcing, Colors, and Care Guide

Hydrangeas are one of the most versatile flowers in professional floristry. A single stem fills space that would take 5-8 stems of most other flowers. For weddings, they're the go-to for creating lush, abundant arrangements on a budget. For retail, they're a premium product that clients actively request.

But hydrangeas are also one of the most temperamental flowers to handle. Get the care wrong and they wilt in hours. This guide covers sourcing, pricing, handling, and design — everything a florist needs to work with hydrangeas profitably.

## Why Hydrangeas Are a Florist Staple

- **Volume per stem:** One hydrangea bloom is 6-8 inches across, replacing multiple stems in an arrangement
- **Color range:** White, blue, purple, pink, green, antique — something for every palette
- **Client demand:** Consistently one of the top 5 most-requested flowers for weddings
- **Perceived value:** Clients see hydrangeas as premium and luxurious
- **Design versatility:** Works as focal, filler, or mass element depending on how you use it

### Color Notes

- **Blue hydrangeas turn purple** if the water is alkaline. Use acidified water (pH 3.5-4.5) to maintain blue color
- **"Antique" refers to color,** not age. Antique hydrangeas have muted, multi-toned petals (burgundy, rust, sage). They're harvested later in the bloom cycle
- **Tinted hydrangeas** are dyed using stem absorption. Common tinted colors: black, teal, orange, rainbow. Higher cost ($4-8/stem) but popular for events with specific color palettes

## Care and Handling — The Critical Guide

Hydrangeas are the most care-sensitive flower in wholesale. Poor handling is the #1 reason florists have issues with them.

### At Receiving (Do This Immediately)

1. **Unpack within 30 minutes** of delivery arrival
2. **Cut stems at an angle** — 2 inches off, at 45 degrees, with a sharp knife
3. **Score the stem.** Make a 1-inch vertical slit up the bottom of the stem. Hydrangea stems are woody and need extra water uptake surface
4. **Remove lower leaves.** Leave only 2-3 leaves near the bloom
5. **Hydrate in warm water** (100-110°F / 38-43°C) with flower food. Warm water is absorbed faster through the woody stems
6. **Mist the blooms.** Hydrangeas drink through their petals as well as their stems. Spray the blooms with water
7. **Store at 36-40°F** (2-4°C) in the cooler. High humidity is essential

### The Hydrangea Emergency Protocol

If hydrangeas arrive wilted (it happens — they're sensitive to dehydration during shipping):

1. **Submerge the entire bloom** in cool water for 30-60 minutes. Yes, the whole flower, underwater
2. **Recut stems** while submerged if possible
3. **After soaking,** shake off excess water and place in warm water with flower food
4. **Mist every 2-3 hours** for the first day
5. **Most hydrangeas recover within 4-8 hours.** If still wilted after 12 hours, they likely won't recover

### Ongoing Care

- **Mist daily.** Hydrangeas lose moisture through petals faster than most flowers
- **Keep water level high.** They're heavy drinkers — check twice daily
- **Change water every 2 days** with fresh flower food
- **Keep cool.** Hydrangeas wilt fastest in heat. Never display near windows or heat sources
- **Don't mix with daffodils.** Daffodil sap is toxic to hydrangeas

## Design Applications

### Wedding Work

Hydrangeas are the budget hero of wedding floristry:

- **Bridal bouquet:** 4-5 white hydrangea heads create a lush, full bouquet at fraction of the cost of an all-rose bouquet
- **Centerpieces:** 2-3 hydrangeas fill a centerpiece that would need 15-20 roses
- **Aisle ends:** Single hydrangea per pew/chair is elegant and affordable
- **Ceremony arch:** Hydrangeas as the "base" with accent roses/ranunculus = full look, lower cost
- **Budget math:** A centerpiece with 3 hydrangeas ($9-12 wholesale) vs. 15 roses ($15-20) + filler ($5) = same visual impact, lower cost

### Retail Arrangements

- **Single-variety:** All white or all blue hydrangeas = striking, modern
- **Classic mix:** Hydrangea + roses + eucalyptus = the arrangement that never fails
- **Summer:** Blue hydrangeas + white roses + greenery = coastal/fresh
- **Fall:** Antique hydrangeas + burgundy roses + autumnal foliage = rich, seasonal

### Corporate/Event

- **Lobby arrangement:** 5-7 hydrangeas in a large vessel = massive impact, reasonable cost
- **Gala centerpiece:** Hydrangea base + premium focal (orchids, garden roses) = luxurious on a budget
- **Conference table:** Low arrangement with 3 green hydrangeas + filler = clean, professional

## Common Mistakes

1. **Not hydrating immediately.** Every minute out of water matters with hydrangeas. Unpack and hydrate within 30 minutes of arrival
2. **Using cold water.** Warm water (not hot) is critical for initial hydration of woody stems
3. **Forgetting to mist.** Hydrangeas absorb water through petals. Misting adds 2-3 days of vase life
4. **Not scoring stems.** The vertical slit increases water uptake significantly
5. **Displaying in heat.** Hydrangeas and heat don't mix. Keep them in the coolest spot available
6. **Ordering too far ahead.** Order hydrangeas 3-5 days before you need them, not a week

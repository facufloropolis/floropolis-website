
# Delphinium Care Guide: Preventing Petal Drop and Getting 10+ Days of Vase Life

Delphiniums are among the most requested flowers for spring and summer weddings — and also one of the most mishandled. The iconic tall spikes with densely packed florets look effortless in arrangements, but delphiniums have a specific set of conditioning requirements that make the difference between a stunning arrangement and one that drops petals all over a wedding table before the ceremony ends.

This guide covers everything you need to know: how to condition delphiniums correctly, the petal drop problem and how to prevent it, what vase life to expect, variety selection, and why year-round availability from Ecuador has changed how florists source this flower.

## The Petal Drop Problem (and Why It Happens)

Delphinium petal drop is the most common complaint florists have with the variety. It looks like this: the lower florets on the spike begin dropping petals — starting at the base and working upward — before the upper buds have fully opened. For events, this means an arrangement can look perfect at delivery and visually compromised by the time guests arrive.

**The causes:**

1. **Bacteria in the stem water.** This is the primary driver. Bacteria colonizes the stem base and interrupts water uptake, causing premature senescence in the lower florets. Delphiniums are particularly susceptible because of the number of florets on a single spike — a bacterial problem that affects 10% of a rose shows up much more obviously on a delphinium.

2. **Temperature fluctuation.** Moving delphiniums between the cooler and a warm design room repeatedly stresses the florets and accelerates petal drop. Delphiniums that have been in cold storage and are then brought to room temperature for design work can start dropping within hours.

3. **Ethylene exposure.** Delphinium florets are highly sensitive to ethylene gas, which is produced by ripening fruit, aging flowers, and decaying plant matter. Any delphinium stored near these sources will drop petals much faster.

4. **Age at sourcing.** Delphiniums sourced through distributors may have 2-3 extra days of transit before they reach you — and by that point, the lower florets are already at peak or past it. You do the arrangement work and the florets drop within 24 hours.

## Conditioning Delphiniums: Step-by-Step

### On Arrival

1. **Unbox immediately** — delphiniums can bruise in transit. Handle carefully: the spike is fragile, especially at the floret attachment points.

2. **Inspect for petal drop** — if lower florets are already dropping, the flowers are past peak. Document with photos. Fresh delphiniums should have firm, intact florets with the lower blooms open and the upper 30-40% still in bud stage.

3. **Remove lower foliage** — strip all foliage below the waterline. Submerged leaves are a bacterial breeding ground.

4. **Re-cut at 45° angle** — 1-2 inches off the bottom with sharp clippers. Delphiniums have hollow stems, which makes them efficient at water uptake when cut correctly and susceptible to air locks when not.

5. **Fill hollow stems with water (pro technique)** — Turn the stem upside down and use a small syringe or squeeze bottle to fill the hollow cavity with water, then plug with a small piece of wet cotton. This eliminates the air pocket in hollow stems and dramatically improves vase life. Not essential for every stem, but worth doing for high-value event work.

6. **Place in clean buckets immediately** — bucket cleanliness is more important for delphiniums than almost any other flower. Use sanitized buckets only.

7. **Add floral preservative** — the biocide component is critical for bacteria prevention.

### Storage

8. **Store at 36-40°F** — slightly warmer than roses but still cold. Do not store with ripening fruit or aging flowers.

9. **Keep away from ethylene sources** — this means no fruit bowls, no warm exhaust vents, no aging arrangements nearby.

10. **Minimize temperature transitions** — if possible, condition once and leave in cold storage until final design. Every cold-to-warm transition stresses the florets.

11. **Change water daily** — more frequently than most flowers. Fresh water with a small amount of bleach (1 drop per liter) is highly effective at bacterial prevention.

## Vase Life: What to Expect

| Condition | Expected Vase Life |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Farm-direct, properly conditioned | 9-12 days |
| Distributor-sourced, properly conditioned | 6-9 days |
| Any source, poor conditioning | 3-5 days (petal drop within 2-3 days) |

The vase life gap between farm-direct and distributor-sourced delphiniums is 3+ days — significant for event work where timing matters.

## Delphinium Design Tips

### Height and Architecture

Delphiniums are the tall accent that gives arrangements vertical movement. Use them in designs that need architectural height:
- Tall glass cylinder centerpieces
- Altar and ceremony installations
- Hotel lobby pieces
- Large-scale wedding arches

A single bundle of 10 Sky Waltz delphiniums in a tall vase is a complete, high-impact design. You don't need to pair them with anything.

### Color Palette Pairings

**Blue delphiniums + white roses + eucalyptus** — the most requested blue-and-white palette. Clean, classic, consistently popular.

**White Galahad + white lisianthus + silver dollar eucalyptus** — pure white palette with textural contrast.

**Blue delphiniums + peonies (when in season) + garden roses** — lush, full summer arrangement style.

**Blue + purple delphinium mix + lavender roses** — moody, romantic palette for intimate wedding settings.

### Scale Planning

Delphiniums are tall flowers (60-90cm). They work in tall vessels — they look undersized in low, wide arrangements.

Planning guide:
- Tall centerpiece: 5-10 stems per arrangement
- Altar arch: 20-40 stems for accent coverage
- Hotel lobby installation: 15-25 stems per piece

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Why are my delphiniums dropping petals immediately?**
A: Most likely causes: bacteria in the water (clean bucket thoroughly), too warm (get them in cold storage faster), or ethylene exposure (keep away from fruit and aging flowers). If they arrived already dropping, contact your supplier — this is a sourcing quality issue.

**Q: Can I use delphiniums in foam?**
A: Yes, but limit their time in foam. Wet floral foam can become a bacterial breeding ground, which accelerates petal drop for delphiniums. For multi-day events in foam, add fresh flower food directly to the foam water and mist the arrangement daily.

**Q: What is the minimum order for wholesale delphiniums?**
A: 10 stems per bunch minimum at Floropolis. Mix multiple varieties in a single order to build your arrangement palette.

**Q: Do blue delphiniums hold their color?**


# Wholesale Roses for Florists: The Complete Buying Guide

Roses account for more than 40% of the wholesale cut flower market in the United States. For most florists, they're the most frequently ordered item — and the one where sourcing quality has the most impact on client satisfaction, waste, and margin.

This guide covers everything professional florists need to know about buying wholesale roses: which varieties to stock, what to pay, how to evaluate freshness and vase life, and why where you source matters as much as what you order.

---

## Understanding the Wholesale Rose Market

### Where Roses Come From

More than 70% of cut roses sold in the US are grown in Ecuador and Colombia. Ecuador, in particular, has become the dominant premium rose supplier due to its ideal growing conditions: high altitude (2,600-3,600 meters), year-round consistent temperatures, and UV-rich sunlight that produces dense, long-lasting blooms.

When you buy wholesale roses through a local distributor, you're most likely buying Ecuador or Colombia roses that have already passed through:
1. Farm (Day 0)
2. Export packing facility (Day 1)
3. International cold storage/freight (Days 1-2)
4. US import/distribution center (Days 3-4)
5. Regional distributor (Days 4-5)
6. Your shop (Days 5-7+)

By the time distributor-sourced roses reach you, 5-7 days of vase life are already used.

**Farm-direct sourcing** bypasses steps 4-5. Roses go from the farm to your shop in 4 days — arriving with 14+ days of usable vase life instead of 10-12.

### Why Vase Life Matters to Your Business

An extra 2-4 days of vase life means:
- Less waste on inventory you don't use before it turns
- More flexibility for event timing (a funeral florist can use an order 5 days later)
- Better client outcomes (their arrangements last longer)
- Higher perceived value (clients notice that Floropolis roses last)

For a florist ordering 500 stems per week, even a 10% reduction in waste pays for the sourcing difference in the first month.

## The 40+ Rose Variety Guide: What to Know

Roses aren't just roses. The variety, growing region, stem length, and bloom stage all affect how a rose performs in your hands. Here's a breakdown of the most important variety categories.

### Classic Red Roses

**Freedom Red** — The standard for premium red roses. Dense, full bloom with 60+ petals, deep red color that holds without darkening. One of Ecuador's most reliably produced roses. Stem lengths: 40cm, 50cm, 60cm.

**Magic Times Red** — Similar profile to Freedom with slightly more vibrant, true-red coloring. Good for Valentine's and event work where saturated color matters.

**Explorer Red** — Slightly larger bloom than Freedom. Strong stem. Preferred for large-scale event work where you need consistent-looking mass.

*Wholesale pricing range: $1.47-$2.93/stem depending on stem length. Freedom 50cm at $2.07/stem is the most commonly ordered.*

### White Roses

**Escimo White** — The industry standard white rose. Pure snow-white petals, high petal count, reliable vase life. Works in every context: weddings, funerals, events, retail.

**Playa Blanca White** — Slightly creamier white than Escimo, with a softer look that reads as more romantic. Popular for high-end wedding work.

**Moonstone White** — Large-headed white with a soft, open bloom. Premium for bridal work.

*Wholesale pricing: $1.40-$1.47/stem (50cm, corrected prices).*

### Garden Roses

**Quicksand (Cream)** — Ecuador's most popular garden-style rose. Soft quartered petals with a blush-cream color and subtle dusty finish. Photographs beautifully. The backbone of garden-style bridal work.

**Antonia Garden (Cream)** — Similar garden aesthetic to Quicksand with slightly warmer cream tones. Fuller bloom.

*These are premium roses — pricing reflects the larger bloom size and higher demand:* $1.47-$1.55/stem (corrected prices).

### Peach and Blush Roses

**Shimmer (Peach Pink)** — Soft peach with pink undertones. The "Instagram rose" — this color palette drives significant Pinterest and wedding board saves.

**Twilight (Peach)** — Warm peach, classic garden color, strong stems. Consistent performer for spring and summer wedding work.

*Wholesale pricing: $1.47-$1.55/stem.*

### Lavender and Mauve Roses

**Arya Lavender** — Soft purple-lavender, one of the most requested non-traditional rose colors for modern wedding work.

**Cool Water Lavender** — True cool-toned lavender. Pairs with white and silver for high-end, modern florals.

*Wholesale pricing: $1.22-$1.38/stem (corrected prices).*

### Orange Roses

**Free Spirit Orange** — The most popular orange rose in the wholesale market. Rich amber-orange with a large bloom and high petal count.

**Cayenne Orange** — Slightly deeper, more saturated orange than Free Spirit. Better for high-contrast fall arrangements.

*Wholesale pricing: $1.32-$1.38/stem.*

### Yellow Roses

**Brighton Yellow** — Classic full yellow rose. High demand for Easter, graduation, and corporate events.

*Wholesale pricing: $1.40/stem.*

## Stem Length Guide: 40cm vs 50cm vs 60cm

Stem length affects both price and use. Here's what you need to know:

| Stem Length | Best Use | Price Difference | Notes |
|------------|---------|-----------------|-------|
| 40cm | Compact arrangements, corsages, small bouquets | Lowest | Some varieties limited at this length |
| 50cm | Standard arrangements, most events, bridesmaids | Middle | Most widely available |
| 60cm | Statement arrangements, tall centerpieces, upright designs | Highest | Better stem strength for tall vessels |

**For most florists:** 50cm is the workhorse. Order 60cm specifically when the design requires it. 40cm for budget-sensitive work or compact designs.

## How to Evaluate Rose Freshness at Arrival

When your wholesale rose order arrives, inspect it before signing off:

**Good signs:**
- Buds are tight or slightly cupped — not fully open
- Petals are firm and slightly waxy to touch
- Stems are straight and rigid (not bending at the head)
- Color is vibrant and even (no browning or discoloration at petal edges)
- Mild, fresh scent

**Problem signs:**
- Open or "blown" blooms on arrival = too old or improper cold storage
- Petals with brown edges = dehydration or ethylene damage
- Soft, mushy stem bases = bacteria from improper storage
- Strong sweet or fermented smell = post-peak flowers

If you receive roses with quality issues, document with photos and contact your supplier within 24 hours.

## Conditioning Roses: The Essentials

1. **Trim 1-2 inches at a 45° angle** under water with sharp clippers (not scissors)
2. **Remove guard petals** — the outermost 2-3 petals are protective and often imperfect. Remove them for cleaner presentation
3. **Strip foliage below waterline** to prevent bacterial growth
4. **Place in clean water with floral preservative** immediately
5. **Store at 34-38°F** for at least 4 hours before using (ideally overnight)

**Expected vase life:** 14-16 days for farm-direct roses properly conditioned. 10-12 days for distributor-sourced.

## Rose Seasonal Planning for Florists

**Valentine's Day (February 14):** Order 3-4 weeks ahead. This is the single highest-demand day for roses in the year. Expect prices to rise 30-50% in the week before Valentine's. Farm-direct suppliers with direct farm relationships can offer better pricing stability.

**Mother's Day (May):** Second highest demand period. Similar advance ordering recommendation.

**Spring wedding season (April-June):** Roses are in peak demand. For garden-style roses (Quicksand, Antonia), availability can tighten. Order early for large events.

**Summer (July-August):** Natural growing conditions are less ideal for some Ecuador farms due to the Southern Hemisphere winter. Availability is consistent but pricing may rise slightly on premium varieties.

**Fall (September-October):** Strong demand returns for fall weddings. Warm-colored roses (peach, orange, yellow) are in highest demand.

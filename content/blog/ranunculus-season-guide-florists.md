## Ranunculus Season: What You Need to Know

### Natural Season

Ranunculus (*Ranunculus asiaticus*) is a cool-season flower. It needs cold nights and mild days to develop properly.

**Natural peak season in the US:** January through April (sometimes extending to May in cooler climates)

**Why it matters:** During natural season, supply is highest and prices are most competitive. Outside of season, ranunculus is available but pricing increases as farms rely on climate-controlled growing conditions.

### Ecuador Year-Round Supply

Ecuador's elevation (2,600–3,600 meters above sea level) creates permanently cool, spring-like conditions year-round. This is why Ecuador has become the dominant source for ranunculus in the US wholesale market — farms there can grow it 52 weeks a year.

**What this means for florists:** If you source from Ecuador-based wholesale suppliers, ranunculus availability is consistent throughout the year. The price advantage is still in January–May (peak season, highest Ecuador farm production), but you're not locked into a hard seasonal window.

## Ranunculus Varieties and Colors for 2026

### Popular Wedding Colors

**White and cream** — The most requested ranunculus color for weddings. White ranunculus photographs beautifully in natural light. Cream varieties (warm white with yellow centers) have a garden-rose quality that clients love.

**Blush pink and peach** — Consistently trending. Pairs with eucalyptus greens, garden roses, and sweet peas for the organic, garden-style bouquets that dominate Instagram.

**Coral and orange** — Bold, rich color for summer and fall events. High contrast against green foliage.

**Red** — Classic and dramatic. Used as accent stems in large wedding installations or as a primary in deep-color bouquets.

**Burgundy and mauve** — Trending for moody, atmospheric wedding aesthetics. Pairs with dark foliage (burgundy hypericum, black-tipped lotus pods).

### Variety Notes

The standard commercial variety (*Ranunculus asiaticus* in various cultivars) is what most wholesale suppliers carry. There are also Italian-grown "picotee" varieties with bi-color petals (white edge with pink or red center) that command premium pricing — if you can source these, they're worth the markup for bespoke bridal work.

## How to Condition Ranunculus

Ranunculus has soft, hollow stems that are more delicate than roses but not as fragile as anemones. The conditioning process is similar to most cut flowers, with a few ranunculus-specific notes.

### On Arrival

1. **Unbox and allow to acclimate** — 15-30 minutes at room temperature before trimming helps prevent condensation shock
2. **Remove plastic sleeve or banding** from stems
3. **Trim 1-2 inches at a 45° angle** — sharp, clean clippers only. Hollow stems crush easily.
4. **Remove any foliage below the waterline** — submerged leaves accelerate bacterial growth
5. **Place in clean water with floral preservative** immediately

### Storage

- **Temperature:** 34-38°F (cold storage)
- **Avoid ethylene** — keep away from ripening fruit and aging flowers
- **Change water every 2 days**
- **Allow to bloom gradually** — ranunculus arrives tight. Let them open naturally in the cooler. This takes 2-4 days from Ecuador farms.

### Opening the Blooms

If you need ranunculus to open faster (last-minute orders, same-day events):
- Move to warmer room temperature (65-70°F) for a few hours
- Avoid direct heat — warmth encourages bloom, heat wilts

## Design Tips: Working with Ranunculus

### Where They Excel

**Garden-style bouquets:** Ranunculus are the backbone of the organic, lush, "just-picked" bouquet style that has dominated weddings for the past five years. They have depth and texture — a dozen open ranunculus stems look like twenty flowers.

**Low, wide centerpieces:** A low oval centerpiece of mixed spring flowers with ranunculus as the primary element is one of the most requested (and highest-margin) wedding centerpiece styles. Pair with garden roses, anemones, sweet peas, and eucalyptus.

**Mixed season arrangements:** In January–April, ranunculus pairs naturally with tulips, narcissus, sweet peas, and delphinium — all peak at the same time, all work together visually.

### Scale and Stem Count

Ranunculus blooms are medium-sized (2-3 inch diameter when open). They're not large enough to be the sole "statement" flower in a large arrangement without significant volume.

Planning guide:
- Compact bouquet: 20-25 stems
- Full bridal bouquet: 30-40 stems
- Centerpiece (low, lush): 25-35 stems per arrangement
- Altar arrangement: 75-120 stems for impact

### What Doesn't Work

Ranunculus doesn't hold up well in foam (floral foam accelerates decay for soft-stemmed flowers). For large installations, use water tubes or design with vases and vessels rather than foam.

## The Best Time to Stock Ranunculus (Calendar)

**January – February:** Start of peak season. Prices at annual low. Stock aggressively for Valentine's Day and early spring weddings.

**March – April:** Peak demand season. Wedding consultation season picks up — florists receive the most ranunculus requests during this window. Keep consistent stock.

**May:** Season winds down in some regions but Ecuador supply stays strong. Pricing remains competitive.

**June – August:** Summer shoulder season. Ecuador supply continues but natural demand is lower (spring wedding season ending). Good time to introduce ranunculus to non-seasonal clients — hospitality, corporate accounts.

**September – December:** Demand builds again for fall weddings and holiday florals (burgundy and coral ranunculus work well in fall color palettes).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can ranunculus be used year-round or only in spring?**
A: When sourced from Ecuador farms, ranunculus is available 12 months a year. Natural season (January–May) offers the best selection and pricing. Summer availability is consistent but selection may be narrower.

**Q: How do I know if ranunculus is fresh when buying?**
A: Look for tight, closed buds. The petals should be firm, not papery. Stems should be firm at the base (not soft or mushy). Fresh ranunculus has a slightly grassy smell; aging flowers develop a sour odor.

**Q: What's the minimum order for ranunculus wholesale?**
A: At Floropolis, the minimum is 20 stems per bunch. You can combine varieties (white, pink, coral) in a single order to hit volume requirements while getting color variety.

**Q: Why is my ranunculus wilting quickly?**
A: The most common causes: bacteria in the bucket (clean thoroughly before each use), stems were cut with scissors instead of clippers (crushing the hollow stem), or the flowers were stored too warm. Review the conditioning steps above.

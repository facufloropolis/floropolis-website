# Wholesale Protea for Florists: Types, Sourcing, and Design Guide

Protea are the statement flowers of modern floral design. A single king protea transforms a $60 arrangement into a $120 centerpiece. They photograph beautifully, they dry perfectly for long-lasting designs, and they're unlike anything else in your cooler.

For florists, protea represent a high-margin specialty category that differentiates your work from every-other-shop's roses-and-carnations approach.

## Care and Handling

### At Receiving
1. **Unpack carefully** — protea blooms are sturdy but can crease if compressed
2. **Cut stems at 45 degrees** — woody stems need a sharp, clean cut
3. **Remove lower leaves** — standard practice
4. **Place in room-temperature water** — protea don't need cold water
5. **Store at 40-50°F** — warmer than standard cooler. Protea are warm-climate plants
6. **NO flower food** — protea are sensitive to flower food chemicals. Use clean water only

### Key Care Points

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Water** | Clean water only. No flower food, no additives |
| **Temperature** | 40-50°F storage (NOT standard 34-38°F cooler — too cold) |
| **Handling** | Stems are woody and sturdy. Blooms are more delicate — don't compress |
| **Ethylene** | Moderately sensitive. Keep away from fruit |
| **Blackening** | Leaves can blacken in cold storage. This is the most common protea problem |
| **Vase life** | 14-21 days with clean water. Exceptional longevity |
| **Drying** | Hang upside down in dry, dark space for 2-3 weeks. They dry perfectly |

### Common Problems

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Blackening leaves | Too cold (below 38°F) | Store warmer. Remove blackened leaves |
| Wilting despite water | Bacterial blockage | Fresh cut, clean water, clean vase |
| Petal browning | Age or handling damage | Normal at end of life. Dry before browning |
| Mold on bloom | Too humid, not enough air circulation | Improve airflow, reduce humidity |

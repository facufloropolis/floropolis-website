# Wholesale Garden Roses for Florists: Varieties, Pricing, and Design Guide

Garden roses are the single most requested flower in wedding floristry. When a bride says "I want romantic, lush, full-blown roses," she means garden roses — not standard hybrid teas. A single Juliet or Patience rose can anchor a $150 bridal bouquet. And at $2-8 wholesale per stem, the margins are exceptional.

This guide covers the varieties that matter, how to source them, and how to build a profitable garden rose program.

## Garden Roses vs. Standard Roses

| Feature | Garden Roses | Standard (Hybrid Tea) Roses |
|---------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Bloom shape** | Full, cupped, many-petaled (40-100+ petals) | Classic pointed bud, fewer petals |
| **Fragrance** | Strong (many are intensely fragrant) | Little to none |
| **Bloom size** | 3-5 inches fully open | 2-3 inches |
| **Petal count** | 40-100+ | 25-40 |
| **Wholesale price** | $2-8/stem | $0.50-2.50/stem |
| **Vase life** | 5-10 days | 7-14 days |
| **Availability** | Year-round (but variety-dependent) | Year-round |
| **Design style** | Romantic, garden, lush | Classic, modern, minimal |

**Key insight:** Garden roses cost 2-4× more than standard roses wholesale, but retail at 3-6× more. Your per-stem margin is higher, not lower.

## Care and Handling

### At Receiving
1. **Unbox immediately** — garden roses are more fragile than standard roses
2. **Cut stems at 45 degrees**, remove 1-2 inches
3. **Strip ALL lower foliage** — at least 60% of leaves
4. **Place in cool water** (not cold) with flower food
5. **Store at 36-40°F** in cooler
6. **Do NOT crowd** — garden rose blooms are larger and damage easily when compressed

### Opening Garden Roses

Garden roses arrive in bud stage and need to open before use:

| Stage | Timing | Use |
|-------|--------|-----|
| **Tight bud** | Arrived today | Don't use yet — too closed |
| **Showing color, starting to open** | 1-2 days after receiving | Good for arrangements that will be displayed 24+ hours before event |
| **Half open** | 2-3 days after receiving | Ideal for most design work |
| **Fully open (blown)** | 3-5 days after receiving | Best for photos and same-day events. Most dramatic |

**To speed opening:** Place in warm water (100°F) in a warm room for 2-4 hours. Check every hour.
**To slow opening:** Keep in cold storage (34-36°F) with minimal light.

### Common Garden Rose Issues

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Won't open | Arrived too tight, stored too cold | Warm water soak, warm room, patience |
| Petals browning at edges | Age, ethylene, temperature swing | Use fresher stock, consistent temp |
| Bent neck | Dehydration during transit | Fresh cut + warm water. If it doesn't recover in 4 hours, it won't |
| Balling (outer petals stuck) | Moisture + cold | Gently peel outer petals, warm gradually |
| Short vase life | Normal (5-7 days is expected) | Set client expectations. Include care card |

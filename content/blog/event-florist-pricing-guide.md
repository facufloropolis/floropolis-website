# Event Florist Pricing: How to Price Wedding and Event Flowers in 2026

Pricing event flowers is the skill that separates profitable florists from busy-but-broke florists. Underprice and you're working 60-hour weeks with nothing to show for it. Overprice and you lose the booking.

This guide covers the formulas, methods, and real examples that professional event florists use to price profitably — whether you're quoting your first wedding or your hundredth gala.

## The Core Formula

**Event arrangement price = (Wholesale flower cost × markup) + Hard goods + Labor + Delivery/Setup**

Each component has its own calculation:

| Component | Calculation | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Flower cost | Wholesale price × quantity | $45 in flowers |
| Flower markup | 2.5-3.5× wholesale | $45 × 3.0 = $135 |
| Hard goods | Vases, foam, wire, ribbon, tape | $12 |
| Labor | Hours × your rate ($25-75/hr) | 0.5 hr × $50 = $25 |
| Delivery/Setup | Per-event fee or hourly | $75 |
| **Total per arrangement** | | **$247** |

## Markup Ranges by Event Type

Not all events deserve the same markup. Here's the industry standard:

| Event Type | Flower Markup | Why |
|-----------|--------------|-----|
| Weddings | 3.0-3.5× | High customization, emotional value, consultation time |
| Corporate galas | 2.5-3.0× | Volume, simpler designs, repeat potential |
| Fundraisers/Non-profit | 2.5-3.0× | Good networking, but don't discount below cost |
| Private parties | 2.5-3.0× | Less consultation than weddings |
| Funerals | 2.0-2.5× | Compassion pricing, quick turnaround |
| Holiday events | 3.0-3.5× | Peak demand, limited flower supply |

**Important:** These markups are on the wholesale cost of flowers only. Labor, hard goods, and delivery are additional charges on top.

## Labor Rate: The Most Under-Charged Component

Most new florists forget to charge for labor — or charge $15/hour when their skills are worth $50+.

### How to Set Your Labor Rate

**Formula:** Target annual income ÷ billable hours per year = hourly rate

| Target Income | Billable Hours/Year | Hourly Rate |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------|
| $50,000 | 1,500 | $33/hr |
| $75,000 | 1,500 | $50/hr |
| $100,000 | 1,500 | $67/hr |
| $150,000 | 1,500 | $100/hr |

**What counts as labor (charge for ALL of it):**
- Consultation and planning (1-3 hours per wedding)
- Design and production (varies by complexity)
- Setup and teardown at venue
- Sourcing and flower processing
- Administrative time (proposals, emails, contracts)

## Pricing Packages vs. Custom Quotes

### Package Pricing (Recommended for Marketing)

Create 3-4 packages at different price points. This anchors the client's expectations and simplifies your sales process.

| Package | Includes | Price Range |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Essential** | Bridal + 2 bridesmaid bouquets + 5 boutonnieres + 8 centerpieces | $1,500-2,500 |
| **Classic** | Essential + ceremony arch/structure + head table + cake flowers | $2,500-4,500 |
| **Luxe** | Classic + aisle markers + cocktail arrangements + additional personal flowers | $4,500-8,000 |
| **Grand** | Full custom design, multiple installations, premium flowers | $8,000-15,000+ |

**Why packages work:**
- Clients self-select their budget range
- You can standardize production for the most popular package
- Upselling is natural ("The Classic adds a ceremony arch for $X more")
- You control the narrative instead of itemizing every stem

### Custom Quotes (For High-End Clients)

For $5,000+ weddings, use the worksheet method and present a detailed proposal. High-end clients expect customization and detailed breakdowns.

## Common Pricing Mistakes

### 1. Not Charging for Consultation Time
A 2-hour consultation + travel time + email follow-up = 4 hours of your time. That's $200 at $50/hour. If you do 20 consultations to book 10 weddings, that's $4,000 in unbilled time.

**Fix:** Charge a consultation fee ($50-150) that applies to the final invoice if they book. This also qualifies serious clients from browsers.

### 2. Forgetting to Price Repurposing
When ceremony flowers move to the reception (ceremony arch → behind the head table), it saves the client money but costs you labor.

**Fix:** Charge a "repurposing fee" ($50-100) for moving and restyling ceremony flowers to the reception.

### 3. Absorbing Flower Price Increases
Flower prices fluctuate, especially around holidays. If you quoted a wedding 6 months ago at February prices but the wedding is in June, your costs may have changed.

**Fix:** Include a clause in your contract: "Flower prices may vary by season. Final pricing is confirmed 4 weeks before the event." Or build in a 10-15% buffer on flower costs.

### 4. Discounting to Win the Booking
Cutting your price by 20% to beat a competitor means cutting your profit by 40-60% (since costs are fixed).

**Fix:** Instead of discounting, offer to adjust the scope: "I can work within a $2,000 budget — here's what that looks like" (fewer centerpieces, simpler designs, seasonal flowers).

### 5. Not Charging for Rentals
If you provide vases, candelabras, arches, or other items, you need to charge for them — even if you own them.

**Fix:** Rental fees: $5-25 per vase, $50-200 for arch structures, $10-50 per candelabra. Include a damage/replacement clause.

## Sourcing for Maximum Margin

Your flower cost is the single biggest lever on your profit margin. Here's how sourcing strategy affects pricing:

| Sourcing Method | Avg Cost/Stem | Best For |
|----------------|--------------|----------|
| Farm-direct wholesale | $0.45-1.50 | Planned events (order 7-10 days ahead) |
| Wholesale market | $0.80-2.50 | Same-week needs, variety hunting |
| Local distributor | $1.00-3.00 | Last-minute fills, convenience |
| Direct import | $0.30-1.00 | High-volume shops, 20+ events/month |

**The math:** A $3,000 wedding using farm-direct flowers at $0.80/stem average instead of distributor flowers at $1.50/stem saves $150-300 in wholesale cost. That goes straight to your bottom line.

### Standing Orders for Busy Seasons

If you're doing 4+ events per month, set up a weekly standing order with your wholesale supplier:
- Consistent quality (your designs look the same every time)
- Volume pricing (5-15% better than one-off orders)
- Less time sourcing (more time designing)
- Predictable costs (better proposals and pricing)

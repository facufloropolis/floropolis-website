# Wholesale Orchids for Florists: Types, Care, and Sourcing Guide

Orchids are the luxury backbone of floral design. No other flower family offers the same combination of elegance, variety, longevity, and profit margin. A single phalaenopsis plant retails for $35-75 from a $12-20 wholesale cost. A cymbidium orchid stem commands $15-25 wholesale and anchors a $150+ arrangement.

This guide covers the orchid types that matter for florists, how to source and care for them, and how to price them for maximum profit.

## Orchid Types for Florists

### Phalaenopsis (Moth Orchid) — The Bestseller

**Why it matters:** Phalaenopsis accounts for 75%+ of all orchid sales in the US. It's the orchid your customers know by sight, even if they can't name it.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Stem count** | 1-3 stems per plant |
| **Bloom count** | 5-15 flowers per stem |
| **Colors** | White, pink, purple, yellow, spotted, striped |
| **Vase life (cut)** | 14-21 days |
| **Plant life** | 3-6 months in bloom, reblooms with care |
| **Wholesale (plant)** | $8-20 (single stem), $15-35 (double), $25-50 (triple) |
| **Wholesale (cut stem)** | $3-8 per stem |
| **Retail** | $25-75 (plant), $15-25 (cut stem in arrangement) |

**Best uses:** Plant gifts, luxury arrangements, corporate accounts, weddings, sympathy

### Dendrobium — The Workhorse

**Why it matters:** The most affordable orchid for cut flower work. Long stems, multiple blooms, excellent vase life.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Stem length** | 12-24 inches |
| **Bloom count** | 8-15 flowers per stem |
| **Colors** | White, purple, green, pink, bi-color |
| **Vase life** | 14-21 days |
| **Wholesale** | $1.50-4.00 per stem |
| **Retail** | $8-15 per stem in arrangement |

**Best uses:** Wedding bouquets, leis, Asian-inspired designs, tropical arrangements, everyday luxury

### Cymbidium — The Statement Maker

**Why it matters:** Large, dramatic blooms that photograph beautifully. The orchid that says "this is a luxury arrangement."

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Bloom size** | 3-5 inches |
| **Blooms per stem** | 8-15 |
| **Colors** | Green, white, pink, burgundy, yellow, brown |
| **Vase life** | 21-28 days |
| **Wholesale** | $8-25 per stem |
| **Season** | Peak: October-April. Limited: May-September |
| **Retail** | $25-50 per stem in arrangement |

**Best uses:** Luxury centerpieces, corsages (individual blooms), corporate events, winter arrangements

### Oncidium (Dancing Lady) — The Texture Element

**Why it matters:** Sprays of small, delicate blooms that add movement and texture. No other flower creates this effect.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Stem length** | 18-30 inches |
| **Bloom count** | 50-100+ tiny flowers per spray |
| **Colors** | Yellow (most common), red, brown, purple, white |
| **Vase life** | 14-21 days |
| **Wholesale** | $3-7 per stem |
| **Retail** | $12-20 per stem in arrangement |

**Best uses:** Cascading arrangements, tropical designs, texture accent, modern installations

### Vanda — The Showstopper

## Care and Handling

### Cut Orchid Stems

**At receiving:**
1. Unpack immediately — orchids are ethylene sensitive
2. Cut stems at 45 degrees, 1 inch
3. Place in clean water with floral preservative
4. Store at 55-65°F (NOT cold cooler — orchids are tropical)
5. Keep upright — stems curve toward light if laid sideways

**Critical:** Orchids should NOT go in your standard 34-38°F cooler. They need 55-65°F. A too-cold cooler causes bud blast (buds drop before opening). Use a separate cool area or the warmest shelf of your cooler.

### Orchid Plants

**At receiving:**
1. Remove from shipping sleeve immediately
2. Check for broken stems — tape gently with floral tape if minor
3. Place in bright, indirect light
4. Water sparingly — orchid roots rot easily
5. Do NOT place on heated or cold surfaces
6. Mist lightly if humidity is low

**Display tip:** Group orchid plants together in your shop. A "orchid bar" display creates a destination within your store and justifies premium pricing.

### Common Orchid Problems

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Buds dropping (bud blast) | Temperature too cold, ethylene exposure | Keep 55-65°F, away from fruit/exhaust |
| Yellow leaves | Overwatering or too much direct sun | Reduce water, move to indirect light |
| Limp/wrinkled stems | Dehydration during transit | Fresh cut + warm water soak (30 min) |
| Brown spots on petals | Cold damage or bacterial | Remove affected blooms, isolate plant |
| Stem snap | Handling damage | Support with wire, use as floating bloom |

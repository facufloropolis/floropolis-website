## Care and Handling

### At Receiving
1. **Cut stems** at 45-degree angle, remove 2 inches
2. **Remove lower foliage** and any yellowing leaves
3. **Place in room temperature water** with flower food
4. **Store at 36-40°F** — lisianthus is cold-tolerant
5. **Don't overcrowd** — lisianthus stems are delicate and blooms bruise if squeezed

### Key Care Points
- **Stems are fragile.** Handle gently. They bend and snap more easily than roses
- **Blooms open sequentially.** Buds on the same stem open over 3-5 days. Buy with some open + some in bud for maximum display life
- **Remove spent blooms.** As individual blooms on a stem fade, remove them to let remaining buds open
- **Avoid ethylene.** Lisianthus is moderately ethylene-sensitive. Keep away from fruit

## Common Mistakes

1. **Treating them like roses.** Lisianthus stems are thinner and more fragile. Don't strip leaves by pulling down — you'll snap the stem. Cut leaves individually
2. **Buying too tight.** Unlike roses, lisianthus buds that are very tight may not open fully. Buy with at least 1-2 blooms already open per stem
3. **Ignoring the doubles.** Single-bloom lisianthus is fine, but double-bloom is where the rose comparison really works. Pay the $0.20-0.30 premium for doubles when doing bridal work
4. **Not removing spent blooms.** One brown bloom on a multi-bloom stem makes the whole thing look tired. Pinch spent blooms to keep it fresh

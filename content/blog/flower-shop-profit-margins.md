# Flower Shop Profit Margins: How to Calculate and Improve Your Margins in 2026

Margins make or break a flower shop. You can have beautiful designs, loyal customers, and a full order book — and still lose money if your margins are wrong.

This guide covers the real numbers: what margins look like in the industry, how to calculate yours, and the specific levers that move your profitability.

## Industry Average Margins

### Gross Margins (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold)

| Revenue Source | Typical Gross Margin | Notes |
|---------------|---------------------|-------|
| Daily retail arrangements | 55-65% | Standard 2.5-3x markup on flowers |
| Wedding & events | 65-80% | Higher markup (3-5x), premium pricing |
| Subscriptions | 50-60% | Lower markup, but predictable recurring revenue |
| Wire service (FTD, Teleflora) | 20-35% | Low margin after fees — many florists are dropping these |
| Corporate accounts | 55-70% | Recurring, predictable, but price-negotiated |

### Net Profit Margins (After All Expenses)

| Shop Size | Typical Net Margin | Annual Revenue | Net Profit |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|------------|
| Home-based florist | 15-25% | $50K-150K | $7.5K-37.5K |
| Small shop (1-2 employees) | 10-15% | $150K-350K | $15K-52.5K |
| Medium shop (3-5 employees) | 8-12% | $350K-750K | $28K-90K |
| Large shop (6+ employees) | 5-10% | $750K+ | $37.5K-75K+ |

**Key insight:** Net margins typically decrease as shop size increases because overhead (rent, labor, utilities) grows faster than revenue. Home-based florists with low overhead often have the highest net margins as a percentage — even though their total revenue is lower.

## How to Calculate Your Margins

### Gross Margin Per Arrangement

**Formula:** (Selling Price - Cost of Goods) / Selling Price × 100

**Example: A dozen Freedom Red Roses**
- Wholesale cost: 12 stems × $0.94 = $11.28
- Hard goods (wrap, ribbon, flower food): $3.00
- **Cost of goods: $14.28**
- Selling price: $55.00
- **Gross margin: ($55.00 - $14.28) / $55.00 = 74%**

### Gross Margin Including Labor

**Formula:** (Selling Price - COGS - Labor) / Selling Price × 100

**Same dozen roses with labor:**
- COGS: $14.28
- Labor: 15 minutes at $20/hour = $5.00
- **Total cost: $19.28**
- Selling price: $55.00
- **Margin with labor: ($55.00 - $19.28) / $55.00 = 65%**

### Effective Margin (Including Waste)

This is the number most florists don't calculate — and it's the one that matters most.

**Formula:** Gross margin × (1 - waste rate)

**If your waste rate is 20%:**
- You bought 100 stems but only sold 80
- Your effective cost per sold stem is 25% higher
- Your 74% gross margin becomes ~67% effective margin

**If your waste rate is 10%:**
- You bought 100 stems and sold 90
- Your effective cost per sold stem is only 11% higher
- Your 74% gross margin becomes ~71% effective margin

**The waste rate is the biggest hidden margin killer in floristry.**

## The 6 Levers That Improve Your Margins

### 1. Reduce Waste (Biggest Impact)

**Industry average waste:** 15-25% of purchased inventory
**Target waste rate:** 8-12%
**Margin impact:** Every 5% reduction in waste adds ~3-4% to your effective gross margin

**How to reduce waste:**
- **Source fresher flowers.** Farm-direct stems with 12-14 days of vase life last longer on your shelf than distributor stems with 5-7 days. This alone can cut waste from 20% to 10-12%.
- **Track what sells.** Log daily sales by variety. Stop ordering what doesn't sell. It sounds obvious but most florists buy on autopilot.
- **Use FIFO (first in, first out).** Use your oldest stems first, always. Simple cooler organization makes this easier.
- **Repurpose aging stems.** Stems that are too old for premium arrangements can be used for small bouquets, sampler bundles, or employee "end of day" arrangements sold at cost.
- **Right-size your orders.** Over-ordering is the #1 cause of waste. Order 10% less than you think you need and supplement with local wholesale for emergencies.

### 2. Source Smarter

**Margin impact:** Switching from distributor to farm-direct for 70-80% of orders saves 20-40% on wholesale costs.

| Your Spend | Distributor Cost | Farm-Direct Cost | Monthly Savings |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| $1,000/month | $1,000 | $650-800 | $200-350 |
| $2,000/month | $2,000 | $1,300-1,600 | $400-700 |
| $5,000/month | $5,000 | $3,250-4,000 | $1,000-1,750 |

**The math is straightforward:** Farm-direct flowers cost less AND last longer (less waste). The combined effect on margins is significant.

### 3. Price Correctly

**The most common mistake:** Underpricing. New florists especially underprice because they feel guilty charging "that much" for flowers.

**Pricing benchmarks:**
| Category | Minimum Markup | Target Markup |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Daily retail | 2.5x | 3.0x |
| Events/weddings | 3.0x | 3.5-5.0x |
| Subscriptions | 2.0x | 2.5x |
| Delivery fee | $15 minimum | $15-25 |

**Remember:** Your markup covers more than just flowers. It covers labor, rent, utilities, insurance, vehicle costs, marketing, and your own salary. A 3x markup on flowers does NOT mean you're making 67% profit — it means you're covering your business costs.

**Price increase strategy:** Raise prices 5-10% annually. If you haven't raised prices in 2+ years, you're losing money to inflation. Most customers won't notice a $5 increase on a $50 arrangement.

### 4. Increase Event/Wedding Revenue

Events and weddings have the highest margins in floristry (65-80% gross). Increasing this as a percentage of your total revenue directly improves overall margins.

**The shift:**
| Revenue Mix | Avg. Gross Margin |
|-------------|------------------|
| 80% retail, 20% events | 58% |
| 60% retail, 40% events | 63% |
| 40% retail, 60% events | 68% |

Even modest growth in event work significantly impacts overall profitability.

### 5. Cut Low-Margin Revenue Sources

**Wire services (FTD, Teleflora):** Most florists make 20-35% gross margin on wire orders after fees. Many make a net loss when labor is included. If wire orders are less than 10% of revenue and losing money, consider dropping them.

**Deep discount arrangements:** If you sell "$19.99 specials" to compete with grocery stores, track the actual margin. It's likely below 30% — and it trains customers to expect your work at that price point.

### 6. Control Overhead

| Expense | % of Revenue (Target) | Common Overspend |
|---------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Rent | 10-15% | >20% (location too expensive for revenue) |
| Labor | 20-25% | >30% (overstaffed for volume) |
| Flowers/supplies | 30-40% | >45% (waste or overpricing from supplier) |
| Utilities + insurance | 3-5% | >8% |
| Marketing | 3-5% | <1% (underinvesting hurts growth) |

**The biggest overhead trap:** Rent. A beautiful storefront in a high-traffic area sounds great, but if rent is 25% of revenue, it's eating your profit. Many successful florists operate from home studios or shared spaces with 0-5% rent costs.

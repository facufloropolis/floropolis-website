## Care and Handling

Gladiolus are relatively easy to care for but have specific needs due to their size and bloom pattern.

### At Receiving
1. **Unpack immediately** — gladiolus stems are gravitropic (bend toward light and upward)
2. **Store UPRIGHT** — like snapdragons, gladiolus will permanently bend if laid flat
3. **Cut stems at 45 degrees** — remove 1-2 inches
4. **Place in deep, warm water** (100-110°F) — gladiolus hydrate best in warm water initially
5. **Add floral preservative** — extends life significantly
6. **Store at 36-40°F** — after initial hydration, move to standard cooler
7. **Remove lowest 1-2 florets** if damaged during shipping

### Key Care Points

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Gravitropism** | Like snapdragons, gladiolus bend upward and toward light. ALWAYS store and transport upright |
| **Sequential blooming** | Bottom florets open first, progress upward over 7-14 days |
| **Remove spent florets** | As lower flowers fade, remove them. Helps upper buds open and keeps stems looking fresh |
| **Heavy stems** | Gladiolus are top-heavy. Ensure vases are heavy/sturdy enough. Add marbles or stones for stability |
| **Water consumption** | Heavy drinkers. Check water daily — tall stems use significant water |
| **Tip removal** | Remove the top 2-3 inches of unopened buds for a cleaner look. This is standard practice for sympathy work |

### Common Problems

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Bent/curved stems | Gravitropism (stored horizontally) | Cannot be corrected. Prevent by ALWAYS storing upright |
| Florets browning | Age, ethylene, temperature | Normal progression. Remove spent florets |
| Stems too tall | Standard gladiolus are 36-60" | Cut to desired height. Design from the top down |
| Won't open | Harvested too tight or too cold | Warm water, warm room, patience. Some stems won't open fully |
| Stem snapping | Forced into foam at wrong angle | Cut stem to fit, don't force. Use chicken wire for tall arrangements |

## Design Applications

### Sympathy (Primary Market)
- **Standing sprays:** Gladiolus provide the essential vertical spine. 8-12 stems per spray
- **Casket sprays:** Gladiolus at the ends for length and height
- **Cross/heart shapes:** White gladiolus fill formal sympathy shapes efficiently
- **Church flowers:** Altar arrangements with tall gladiolus are traditional and dramatic
- **Tip:** Always remove the top bud cluster for sympathy work — it gives a cleaner, more formal line

### Large-Scale Events
- **Ceremony arrangements:** Gladiolus in tall urns flanking an altar = dramatic and affordable
- **Buffet backdrops:** Tall gladiolus arrangements behind food stations
- **Church decorations:** Traditional church florals almost always include gladiolus for height

### Summer Seasonal
- **"Glad gardens":** Mixed gladiolus varieties in a large vase = a colorful summer statement
- **Farm-stand aesthetic:** Gladiolus in galvanized buckets = rustic summer display
- **Farmers market:** Gladiolus are top sellers at summer markets — recognized, affordable, impressive

### Modern/Contemporary
- **Single-color column:** 10 gladiolus of one color in a tall cylinder = modern, structural, simple
- **Green gladiolus + white orchids** = contemporary luxury arrangement
- **Mini gladiolus in grid arrangements** = modern, architectural

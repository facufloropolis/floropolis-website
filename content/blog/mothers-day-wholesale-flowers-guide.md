# Mother's Day Wholesale Flowers: The Complete Florist's Planning Guide (2026)

Mother's Day is the second-biggest flower holiday of the year — and for many florists, it's the most profitable weekend. In 2025, Americans spent over $3.6 billion on flowers for Mother's Day alone. As a florist, your success on May 11th starts with what you order today.

This guide covers everything you need to plan your Mother's Day wholesale inventory: which varieties sell best, when to order, how much to order, and how to price arrangements for maximum margin.

## When to Start Ordering

**6 weeks out (April 1):** Lock in your rose order. Ecuador farms allocate inventory early for Mother's Day — if you wait, you'll pay premium or get short stems.

**4 weeks out (April 14):** Order specialty stems (ranunculus, peonies if available, delphiniums). These sell out faster than roses at the wholesale level.

**2 weeks out (April 28):** Final greens and filler order. Eucalyptus, ruscus, and baby's breath don't need as much lead time but you still want to secure your quantities.

**1 week out (May 5):** Confirm all deliveries. Check tracking. Have a backup plan if anything is delayed.

## Pricing Your Mother's Day Arrangements

Standard industry markup for Mother's Day: 3x-4x wholesale cost.

| Arrangement | Cost of Goods | Suggested Retail | Your Margin |
|-------------|--------------|-----------------|-------------|
| Dozen Red Roses (wrapped) | ~$14 (12 stems + wrap) | $45-65 | 69-78% |
| Premium Mixed Bouquet | ~$22 (roses + ranunculus + greens) | $75-95 | 71-77% |
| Luxury Arrangement (vase) | ~$35 (20+ stems + vase) | $120-150 | 71-77% |
| Tropical Statement | ~$30 (heliconias + anthuriums + greens) | $100-130 | 70-77% |

**Key principle:** Mother's Day customers are less price-sensitive than everyday buyers. Don't discount. This is premium territory.

## How Much to Order (Rule of Thumb)

Take your average weekly flower revenue, multiply by 3-5x. That's your Mother's Day weekend target.

**Example:** If you typically do $2,000/week in flower sales:
- Mother's Day target: $6,000-10,000
- Wholesale flower budget: $1,500-3,000 (at 3-4x markup)
- Rose order: ~60-70% of budget = $1,000-2,100 in roses
- That's approximately 1,000-2,200 rose stems at ~$0.94/stem

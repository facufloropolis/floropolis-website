# Wholesale Carnations for Florists: The Underrated Profit Flower

Carnations have an image problem. Many florists dismiss them as "cheap" or "old-fashioned." But here's what the numbers say:

- **Carnations are the #1 selling cut flower in the world** by volume
- **Vase life: 14-21 days** — longer than roses, peonies, or most premium flowers
- **Wholesale cost: $0.25-0.80/stem** — the highest margin flower in your cooler
- **Waste rate: under 5%** — compared to 15-20% for most other flowers

The florists who are making money on carnations aren't selling them as "carnation bouquets." They're using them strategically — as filler, texture, and volume — in arrangements that retail for $50-100+.

## Making Carnations Look Premium

The key to profitable carnation use is design technique. Here's how top florists make carnations look expensive:

### Technique 1: Mass Grouping
Instead of scattering individual carnations through an arrangement, group 8-12 of the same color tightly together. A mass of carnations creates a cloud-like texture that reads as luxurious.

### Technique 2: Monochromatic
All-white carnation arrangements in a clean vessel look modern and sophisticated. Add a single variety of greenery (eucalyptus or ruscus) for contrast.

### Technique 3: Ombré
Grade carnation colors from dark to light across an arrangement: deep burgundy → medium pink → blush → white. This creates a designer effect from the most affordable flower available.

### Technique 4: Carnation "Cake" Installation
Layer carnations tightly in a cylindrical vessel, top fully covered with blooms. This creates a dense, peony-like effect. Instagram-worthy at $10-15 in flowers.

### Technique 5: Mixed Texture
Combine standard carnations (for volume) with spray carnations (for texture) and ruffled varieties (for drama). Three textures of the same flower = visual interest.

## Care and Handling

Carnations are among the easiest flowers to maintain:

### At Receiving
1. Cut stems at 45-degree angle (between nodes, not at a node)
2. Remove foliage below waterline
3. Place in room temperature water with flower food
4. Store at 34-38°F (1-3°C)

### Key Facts
- **Ethylene sensitive:** Keep away from fruit, exhaust, and smoke. Use ethylene inhibitor (STS) if available
- **Water temperature:** Room temp. Carnations aren't fussy
- **Flower food:** Use it. The sugar extends vase life significantly
- **Recut:** Every 3-4 days when changing water

## Design Applications

### Sympathy and Funeral
- **Standing spray:** White standards as the base, with lily or rose focal. Carnations provide the structure at fraction of the cost
- **Casket piece:** Mixed white carnations + greenery for a full, respectful display
- **Cross/heart shape:** Carnations are the ideal flower for shaped tributes — they pack tightly and hold shape

### Wedding
- **Budget bridal bouquet:** Massed white carnations look like peonies when grouped tightly. $8-12 in flowers vs. $35-50 for actual peonies
- **Centerpieces:** Low carnation mass in a gold vessel = $4-6 in flowers, retails for $35-50
- **Aisle markers:** Single carnation "pom" per chair/pew = charming, affordable
- **Installation art:** 500+ carnations massed in an ombré wall = $150-200 in flowers, creates a $2,000+ visual impact

### Everyday Retail
- **Mixed bouquet filler:** 3-5 carnation stems add volume to any arrangement
- **Grab-and-go wraps:** 10 spray carnations in paper = $4 wholesale, $12-15 retail
- **DIY bucket:** Bunches of single-color carnations for DIY customers. $3-5 per bunch

### Corporate
- **Weekly arrangements:** Carnation base + premium focal stem = $3-5 in flowers for a $35-55 weekly arrangement
- **Reception desk:** Single-color standard carnations = cheerful, professional, lasts 2+ weeks

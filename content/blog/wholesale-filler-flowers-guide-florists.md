# Wholesale Filler Flowers for Florists: Types, Uses, and Sourcing Guide

Filler flowers are the unsung heroes of floral design. They complete arrangements, add texture, fill gaps, and transform a handful of focal flowers into a full, professional design. Without them, arrangements look sparse. With the right fillers, they look lush and intentional.

This guide covers every filler flower a florist should know, when to use each, and how they affect your bottom line.

## The Filler Flower Lineup

## The Filler Strategy: Which to Stock

### Always in Your Cooler
- **Waxflower** — the universal filler that works in everything
- **Baby's breath** — classic + trending, cheap, long-lasting
- **One berry/texture filler** — hypericum (fall/winter) or solidago (spring/summer)

### Seasonal Additions
- **Spring/Summer:** Astilbe, Queen Anne's lace
- **Fall:** Solidago, hypericum berries, dried limonium
- **Winter:** Waxflower, hypericum, baby's breath

### For Specific Design Styles
- **Garden/romantic:** Astilbe + waxflower + Queen Anne's lace
- **Modern/minimal:** Baby's breath (single type) or none (negative space instead)
- **Rustic/wildflower:** Solidago + limonium + aster
- **Luxury:** Astilbe + waxflower only (skip budget fillers)

## Care Tips (General)

Most fillers follow similar care rules:

1. **Unpack and process same-day** — fillers dehydrate quickly in boxes
2. **Strip lower foliage** — especially waxflower (leaves rot in water)
3. **Cool water with preservative** — standard treatment
4. **Store at 36-40°F** — standard cooler
5. **Use first, design around** — place fillers in the arrangement first to create the framework, then add focal flowers

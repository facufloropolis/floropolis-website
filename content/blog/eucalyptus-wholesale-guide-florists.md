# Eucalyptus Wholesale Guide: Varieties, Pricing, and Care for Florists

Eucalyptus is the single most versatile green in modern floristry. It works in every arrangement style — rustic, modern, romantic, minimal, tropical. It smells incredible. It dries beautifully. And at wholesale prices starting from $0.65/stem, it's one of the highest-value additions to any design.

This guide covers the main eucalyptus varieties available wholesale, how to care for them, and how to use them in your arrangements.

## Eucalyptus Care Guide

### On Arrival

1. **Unpack immediately.** Don't leave eucalyptus in shipping boxes.
2. **Cut stems at an angle.** Fresh cut, 1-2 inches off the bottom.
3. **Remove lower leaves.** Strip leaves that would be submerged in water — they rot and contaminate the water.
4. **Place in clean water.** Room temperature. No flower food needed (but it doesn't hurt).
5. **Store at 34-38°F (1-3°C)** if not using immediately.

### Vase Life Tips

- **Change water every 3-4 days.** Eucalyptus oils make the water murky faster than most flowers.
- **Keep away from direct sunlight.** Eucalyptus holds up well but direct sun speeds dehydration.
- **No ethylene sensitivity.** Eucalyptus is not affected by ethylene gas, so it's safe to store near fruit or other ethylene-producing items.
- **Misting is unnecessary.** The waxy leaf coating retains moisture naturally.

### Drying Eucalyptus

Eucalyptus dries beautifully and maintains its shape. Two methods:

**Air drying:** Hang stems upside down in a dry, dark area for 2-3 weeks. Leaves will curl slightly and color will mute to a grey-green.

**Glycerin preserving:** Mix 1 part glycerin with 2 parts hot water. Place stems in the solution for 1-2 weeks. Leaves stay soft and flexible, color deepens.

## Design Ideas

### Eucalyptus-Only Arrangements
A vase of mixed eucalyptus varieties (Baby Blue + Silver Dollar + Seeded) is a $25-40 retail product that costs $4-6 in materials. Offer it as an "everyday green" option for clients who want something simple and long-lasting.

### Eucalyptus + Roses
The classic combination. Baby Blue eucalyptus with white Mondial roses = timeless elegance. With pink Floyd roses = vibrant and modern. With Freedom red roses = dramatic and romantic.

### Wedding Garlands
Silver Dollar eucalyptus is the #1 requested garland green. At $0.75/stem and 5-8 stems per foot, a 20-foot reception table garland costs $75-120 in wholesale greens. Retail at $25-35/foot = $500-700 retail price. That's a single product generating $380-580 in gross profit.

### Eucalyptus Wreaths
Seeded or Baby Blue eucalyptus formed into a 14-18" wreath. Wholesale cost: ~$5-8. Retail: $35-50. Offer as an add-on to event work or a standalone seasonal product.

## Ordering Tips

1. **Order weekly.** Eucalyptus arrives fresh and lasts 2+ weeks, so weekly ordering gives you consistent supply without waste.
2. **Mix varieties.** Don't just stock Baby Blue. Having 2-3 varieties gives you design flexibility and differentiates your work.
3. **Buy enough.** Eucalyptus goes in almost every arrangement. If you make 20 arrangements/week using 3 stems each, that's 60 stems/week minimum. Order 70-80 to account for waste and bonus arrangements.
4. **Store upright.** Unlike most flowers, eucalyptus stores best upright in water, not laid flat.

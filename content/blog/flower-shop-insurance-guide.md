# Flower Shop Insurance: Types, Costs, and What Every Florist Needs

One broken vase at a $50,000 wedding. A delivery driver rear-ends a car. A customer trips over a bucket in your shop. A cooler fails overnight and $3,000 in flowers are destroyed. Without the right insurance, any of these scenarios could financially devastate your business.

This guide covers every insurance type flower shops need, what it costs, and how to avoid paying for coverage you don't need.

## Insurance Types for Flower Shops

### General Liability Insurance (REQUIRED)

**What it covers:** Injury to customers or damage to their property while in your shop or caused by your products.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Annual cost** | $400-1,200/year |
| **Coverage** | $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate (standard) |
| **Examples** | Customer slips on wet floor, arrangement damages venue table, allergic reaction to flowers |
| **Required?** | Legally required for most commercial leases. Essential for event/wedding work |

**Florist-specific risk:** Venue damage. If your team damages a wedding venue (water stains, candle wax, fallen installation), general liability covers it.

### Commercial Property Insurance

**What it covers:** Your shop space, equipment, inventory, and fixtures against fire, theft, weather, and other perils.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Annual cost** | $500-2,500/year |
| **Coverage** | Replacement value of your property |
| **Key items** | Walk-in coolers ($5,000-15,000), display fixtures, POS equipment, tools |
| **Important** | Make sure policy covers REFRIGERATION BREAKDOWN — cooler failure is the #1 property risk for florists |

**Florist-specific risk:** Cooler failure. A broken cooler overnight can destroy $2,000-10,000 in perishable inventory. Standard property insurance may NOT cover this unless you add equipment breakdown coverage.

### Commercial Auto Insurance (IF YOU DELIVER)

**What it covers:** Vehicles used for business purposes — delivery vans, personal cars used for delivery.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Annual cost** | $1,200-4,000/year per vehicle |
| **Coverage** | Liability + collision + comprehensive |
| **Critical note** | Your personal auto policy does NOT cover business delivery. If you're delivering in a personal car, you need commercial auto or a business-use endorsement |

**Florist-specific risk:** Delivery accidents. If your delivery driver causes an accident during a delivery, your personal auto insurance will likely deny the claim because the vehicle was being used for business.

### Workers' Compensation Insurance

**What it covers:** Employee injuries on the job — cuts from thorns, back injuries from lifting, repetitive strain, vehicle accidents.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Annual cost** | $500-3,000/year (varies by state and payroll) |
| **Required?** | Required in most states once you have 1+ employees |
| **Common claims** | Thorn pricks (infection), slip on wet floor, lifting injury, repetitive strain from design work |

**Florist-specific risk:** Thorn injuries and repetitive strain. Rose thorns cause infections, and hundreds of hours of design work creates hand/wrist strain. Workers' comp covers medical costs and lost wages.

### Product Liability Insurance

**What it covers:** Claims that your product (flowers/arrangements) caused harm.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Annual cost** | Usually included in general liability |
| **Examples** | Allergic reaction, toxic plant ingested by child or pet, arrangement falls and injures someone |
| **Important for** | Event florists — your installations must be safely secured |

### Business Owner's Policy (BOP)

**What it is:** A bundled policy combining general liability + commercial property + (sometimes) business interruption insurance at a discounted rate.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Annual cost** | $1,000-3,000/year |
| **Savings** | 15-30% less than buying policies separately |
| **Best for** | Most flower shops — covers your core risks in one policy |

**Recommendation:** For most flower shops, a BOP is the best starting point. Add commercial auto and workers' comp separately.

### Event/Wedding-Specific Insurance

| Coverage | What It Covers | Cost |
|----------|---------------|------|
| **Event liability** | Damage at the venue during your setup/teardown | Often within GL, confirm limits |
| **Inland marine** | Flowers and equipment in transit to events | $200-500/year |
| **Professional liability (E&O)** | Claims that your design wasn't what was promised | $300-800/year |

**Important for wedding florists:** Many venues require a Certificate of Insurance (COI) naming them as "additional insured." Your insurance company can issue this at no extra cost — but you need to request it for each venue.

## Cost Summary

### Small Shop (1-2 people, under $200K revenue)

| Insurance | Annual Cost |
|-----------|------------|
| Business Owner's Policy (GL + Property) | $1,000-2,000 |
| Commercial Auto (1 vehicle) | $1,200-2,500 |
| Workers' Comp (if applicable) | $500-1,000 |
| **Total** | **$2,700-5,500/year** |

### Medium Shop (3-8 people, $200K-500K revenue)

| Insurance | Annual Cost |
|-----------|------------|

## How to Save on Insurance

| Strategy | Savings |
|----------|---------|
| **Bundle into a BOP** | 15-30% vs. separate policies |
| **Increase deductibles** | Higher deductible = lower premium. Set deductible at what you can afford out-of-pocket |
| **Shop 3+ quotes** | Pricing varies 20-40% between carriers for identical coverage |
| **Pay annually** | Most carriers offer 5-10% discount for annual vs. monthly payment |
| **Safety program** | Document safety procedures → lower workers' comp rates |
| **Claims-free discount** | Stay claims-free for 3+ years → significant premium reduction |
| **Use a niche broker** | Brokers who specialize in florist/retail insurance know the carriers with best rates |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence |
|---------|------------|
| **Using personal car for delivery without commercial coverage** | Claim denied if accident during delivery |
| **Not covering cooler breakdown** | $2,000-10,000 inventory loss not covered |
| **Skipping workers' comp** | Fines + personal liability for employee injuries |
| **Not getting COIs for venues** | Venues won't let you set up, lose the event contract |
| **Underinsuring inventory** | During Valentine's/Mother's Day, your inventory is 3-5× normal. Policy limits may be too low |
| **No inland marine for events** | $5,000 in arrangements in your van gets damaged in transit = your loss |

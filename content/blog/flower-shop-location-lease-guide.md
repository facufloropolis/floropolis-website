# Flower Shop Location & Lease Guide: How to Choose the Right Space

Location can make or break a flower shop. A beautiful shop on the wrong street struggles for foot traffic. A modest shop on a busy corner thrives. Before you sign a lease, you need to understand what makes a location work for a florist — and it's different from most retail.

This guide covers how to evaluate locations, negotiate leases, plan your build-out, and decide whether you even need a retail storefront.

## The First Decision: Do You Need a Storefront?

Before choosing a location, decide your business model:

| Model | Walk-In Revenue | Monthly Rent | Best For |
|-------|----------------|-------------|----------|
| **Full retail storefront** | 40-70% of revenue | $1,500-6,000+ | Established florists wanting foot traffic |
| **Studio/workshop** | 0-10% of revenue | $800-2,500 | Event/wedding florists, delivery-focused |
| **Home-based** | 0% | $0 (zoning permitting) | Startups testing the market |
| **Shared/pop-up** | Variable | $200-1,000 | Testing locations before committing |

### Home-Based: When It Makes Sense

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Zero rent | Zoning restrictions (check local laws) |
| Low overhead while building clientele | No walk-in traffic |
| Tax deduction (home office) | Professional perception challenges |
| Flexible hours | Limited cooler/storage space |

**Start home-based if:** You're new, revenue is under $50K/year, and most orders are delivery or events. Move to a studio when you outgrow your home cooler.

### Studio: The Middle Ground

A studio space (not customer-facing retail) gives you:
- Proper cooler space
- Design workspace
- Professional address
- 50-70% less rent than retail

**Best for:** Wedding and event florists who get most orders online/by phone and deliver everything.

## Choosing a Retail Location

### The 5 Non-Negotiables

| Factor | Why It Matters |
|--------|---------------|
| **1. Foot traffic** | Walk-ins account for 30-50% of retail flower shop revenue. No foot traffic = no impulse buys |
| **2. Visibility** | Can people see your shop from the street? Signage visibility = free advertising |
| **3. Parking** | Customers buying flowers need to carry them. No parking = lost sales |
| **4. Complementary neighbors** | Near restaurants, boutiques, gift shops, grocery stores = people already in buying mode |
| **5. Delivery logistics** | Can your delivery van park/load easily? Is the location central to your delivery zone? |

### Location Types Ranked

| Location | Foot Traffic | Rent | Walk-In Potential | Delivery Access |
|----------|-------------|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Main Street/downtown** | High | High ($3-8/sq ft) | Excellent | Moderate (parking can be difficult) |
| **Strip mall/shopping center** | High | High ($2-6/sq ft) | Very good | Good |
| **Neighborhood/residential** | Low-moderate | Low ($1-3/sq ft) | Moderate (loyal locals) | Excellent |
| **Near hospital** | Moderate | Moderate | Very good (sympathy + get-well) | Good |
| **Near wedding venues** | Low | Low-moderate | Low (but referrals are high) | Good |
| **Farmers market area** | Seasonal high | Varies | Good (weekend traffic) | Good |

### The Foot Traffic Test

Before signing a lease, count actual foot traffic:

1. **Stand outside the location** on a Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday
2. **Count people walking past** during business hours (9 AM - 6 PM)
3. **Note the pattern:** When is it busiest? Who's walking by (demographics)?
4. **Talk to neighboring businesses.** Ask: "What's your foot traffic like? What days are busiest?"
5. **Minimum viable traffic:** 50+ people per hour during business hours for a retail shop

## Space Requirements

### How Much Space Do You Need?

| Shop Size | Square Footage | Monthly Rent (typical) | Revenue Capacity |
|-----------|---------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| **Micro** | 300-500 sq ft | $800-2,000 | Under $10K/month |
| **Small** | 500-800 sq ft | $1,500-3,500 | $10-25K/month |
| **Medium** | 800-1,500 sq ft | $2,500-6,000 | $25-50K/month |
| **Large** | 1,500-3,000 sq ft | $4,000-12,000 | $50K+/month |

### Space Allocation

| Area | % of Total Space | Purpose |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Sales floor** | 30-40% | Customer-facing display and checkout |
| **Design workspace** | 25-35% | Where arrangements are made |
| **Cooler(s)** | 15-20% | Walk-in cooler and/or display cooler |
| **Storage** | 10-15% | Supplies, vases, packaging, seasonal decor |
| **Office/admin** | 5-10% | Desk, computer, phone area |

### Cooler Requirements

The cooler is the most important infrastructure element:

| Type | Cost | Size | Best For |
|------|------|------|----------|
| **Reach-in display cooler** | $2,000-5,000 | 2-4 door | Small shops, limited space |
| **Walk-in cooler (small)** | $5,000-10,000 | 6×6 to 8×8 ft | Most shops |
| **Walk-in cooler (large)** | $10,000-20,000 | 8×10+ ft | High-volume shops |
| **Glass display cooler** | $3,000-8,000 | Varies | Street-facing display (drives walk-ins) |

**Critical:** Budget for cooler installation when evaluating a space. Many spaces don't have the electrical capacity (208/230V) or drainage for a walk-in cooler without upgrades.

## Lease Negotiation

### Key Lease Terms to Negotiate

| Term | What to Negotiate | Why |
|------|------------------|-----|
| **Base rent** | 10-15% below asking. Market is often negotiable | Landlords expect negotiation. If they say no, you know the floor |
| **Lease length** | Start with 2-3 year lease, not 5+ | You need flexibility early. Longer lease = harder to exit if location doesn't work |
| **Build-out allowance (TI)** | Ask for $10-30/sq ft in tenant improvement allowance | Landlord contributes to your build-out in exchange for longer lease |
| **Rent escalation** | Cap annual increases at 2-3% | Some leases have 5%+ escalations. This kills profitability over time |
| **First month free** | Ask for 1-3 months free rent during build-out | You're not generating revenue during construction. Don't pay rent on unusable space |
| **Signage rights** | Confirm you can install exterior signage | Some landlords restrict signage. Visibility = essential |
| **Operating hours** | Confirm you can operate early mornings and holidays | Florists need to prep at 5 AM before Valentine's Day. Make sure the lease allows it |
| **Exclusive use clause** | No other flower shop in the same shopping center | Protects you from direct competition in your immediate area |

### Red Flags in a Lease

| Red Flag | Why It's a Problem |
|----------|-------------------|
| No build-out allowance on a 5+ year lease | Landlord isn't investing in your success |
| Triple net (NNN) with uncapped expenses | You pay all property taxes, insurance, and maintenance. Costs can spike unpredictably |
| Personal guarantee required | If business fails, YOU personally owe the remaining rent. Try to limit to 1 year of rent |
| No early termination clause | If the location doesn't work, you're stuck paying until lease ends |
| Rent above 10% of projected revenue | Industry rule: rent should be under 10% of gross revenue |

### The 10% Rule

**Total occupancy costs (rent + utilities + insurance + maintenance) should not exceed 10-12% of gross revenue.**

| Monthly Revenue | Max Monthly Rent |
|----------------|-----------------|
| $10,000 | $1,000-1,200 |
| $20,000 | $2,000-2,400 |
| $40,000 | $4,000-4,800 |
| $60,000 | $6,000-7,200 |

If rent exceeds this threshold, either negotiate down, find a cheaper location, or increase revenue before signing.

## Build-Out Costs

### Typical Build-Out Budget

| Item | Cost Range |
|------|-----------|
| Walk-in cooler (installed) | $5,000-20,000 |
| Display cooler | $2,000-8,000 |
| Electrical upgrades (for coolers) | $1,000-5,000 |
| Plumbing (sink for design area) | $1,000-3,000 |
| Flooring | $1,000-5,000 |
| Paint and walls | $500-2,000 |
| Lighting | $500-3,000 |
| Signage (exterior) | $1,000-5,000 |
| Display fixtures and shelving | $1,000-5,000 |
| Point of sale system | $150-2,000 |
| Design tables/workspace | $500-2,000 |
| **Total** | **$13,650-60,000** |

**Negotiate tenant improvement (TI) allowance** to offset build-out costs. $15-30/sq ft TI on a 1,000 sq ft space = $15,000-30,000 contribution from the landlord.

## Your Next Step

1. **Decide your model** — Do you need retail, studio, or can you start from home?
2. **Set your rent budget** — Use the 10% rule based on realistic revenue projections
3. **Scout 5-10 locations** — Visit in person, count foot traffic, talk to neighbors
4. **Get a commercial real estate agent** — They represent you (usually free to tenants)
5. **Budget for build-out** — Cooler + plumbing + electrical are the big items

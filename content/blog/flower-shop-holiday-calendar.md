# Flower Shop Holiday Calendar: Revenue Opportunities for Every Month

The difference between a struggling flower shop and a thriving one often comes down to planning. The shops that plan 6-8 weeks ahead for every holiday capture 2-3× more revenue than those scrambling last minute.

This calendar covers every significant flower-selling holiday, with prep timelines, inventory recommendations, and marketing strategies.

## The Big Two: Valentine's Day and Mother's Day

### Valentine's Day (February 14)

**Revenue impact:** 25-35% of February revenue happens on February 13-14 alone. For many shops, this is the single biggest revenue day of the year.

**The Timeline:**

| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| **December** | Lock in wholesale flower contracts. Reserve roses (prices spike in Feb) |
| **January 1** | Launch pre-order campaign on website and Instagram |
| **January 15** | Email existing clients with early-bird pricing |
| **February 1** | Final pre-order push. Order deadline: Feb 8-10 |
| **February 7** | Receive bulk flower delivery. Begin processing |
| **February 11-12** | Design pre-orders. Hire temp help if needed |
| **February 13** | Delivery day for corporate and early personal orders |
| **February 14** | All-hands day. Deliver remaining, handle walk-ins |
| **February 15** | Rest. Debrief. Track what sold, what didn't |

**Inventory recommendations:**
- Red roses: 2-3× your normal weekly order
- Mixed roses: 2× normal
- Greenery: 2× normal
- Chocolates/stuffed animals/vases: Stock 2-4 weeks before
- Packaging: Double your normal supply

**Pricing:** Mark up 20-40% above normal retail pricing for V-Day. This is industry standard and customers expect it.

### Mother's Day (2nd Sunday in May)

**Revenue impact:** The biggest revenue WEEK (not just day) of the year. Demand starts the Friday before and peaks Saturday-Sunday.

**The Timeline:**

| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| **March** | Begin marketing: "Pre-order for Mother's Day" posts on Instagram |
| **April 1** | Launch pre-order page on website |
| **April 15** | Email blast to client list. Early-bird discount ($5-10 off pre-orders) |
| **May 1** | Final pre-order push. Order deadline for guaranteed delivery |
| **May (Mon before)** | Receive bulk delivery. All-hands processing |
| **May (Thu-Fri before)** | Design pre-orders. Prep Saturday walk-in inventory |
| **May (Saturday)** | Peak walk-in day. Full staff |
| **May (Sunday)** | Delivery day. Final orders |

**Inventory:** Similar to Valentine's but with more variety:
- Pink roses: 2-3× normal
- Mixed spring flowers: 2× normal (peonies if in season, hydrangeas, tulips)
- Plants (orchids, peace lilies): High demand as gifts
- Premium wrapping and gift packaging

## Month-by-Month Opportunities

### January: Recovery + Planning
- **New Year's arrangements** (Jan 1-2): Corporate accounts, fresh starts
- **After-holiday sales**: Clear seasonal inventory at 30-50% off
- **Planning time**: Annual review, set goals, update website, plan the year
- **Revenue potential:** Low. Use this month to prepare

### February: Valentine's Day
- See detailed section above
- **Revenue potential:** Highest single-day revenue

### March: Spring Awakening
- **International Women's Day (March 8):** Growing in US. Corporate gift orders
- **St. Patrick's Day (March 17):** Green arrangements, dyed carnations
- **Spring equinox:** Launch spring collection
- **Revenue potential:** Moderate. Good for building momentum

### April: Easter + Admin Week
- **Easter (varies, usually April):** Church flowers, family dinner centerpieces, spring arrangements
- **Administrative Professionals Day (last Wed):** Corporate delivery orders for office assistants/admins
- **Revenue potential:** Moderate-High. Admin Professionals Day is underrated — corporate orders are high-margin

### May: Mother's Day + Weddings
- See detailed section above
- **Wedding season begins:** Consultations ramp up
- **Memorial Day (last Mon):** Cemetery flowers, patriotic arrangements
- **Revenue potential:** Highest week of the year

### June: Weddings + Graduation
- **Father's Day (3rd Sunday):** Lower volume than Mother's Day but growing. Plants, succulents, non-traditional arrangements
- **Graduation:** Party arrangements, congratulations bouquets
- **Wedding peak:** June is the most popular wedding month
- **Revenue potential:** High (from wedding work especially)

### July: Summer + Celebrations
- **Independence Day (July 4):** Red/white/blue arrangements for events and parties
- **Summer parties:** Backyard party centerpieces, outdoor event work
- **Revenue potential:** Moderate

### August: Back to School + Late Summer
- **Teacher gifts:** Small arrangements for first day of school
- **Summer's end events:** Last hurrah outdoor events
- **Revenue potential:** Low-Moderate. Good time for vacations and prep

### September: Fall Begins
- **Grandparents Day (1st Sunday after Labor Day):** Growing holiday. Arrangements delivered to grandparents
- **Rosh Hashanah (varies):** White and yellow flowers, round centerpieces
- **First fall arrangements:** Transition your displays to warm autumn tones
- **Revenue potential:** Moderate

### October: Fall Peak
- **Sweetest Day (3rd Saturday):** Midwest especially. Similar to V-Day but smaller
- **Halloween (Oct 31):** Themed arrangements, corporate displays
- **Día de los Muertos prep:** Marigolds, chrysanthemums (Nov 1-2 but shop early)
- **Fall wedding peak:** September-October is peak fall wedding season
- **Revenue potential:** Moderate-High

### November: Holidays Begin
- **Día de los Muertos (Nov 1-2):** Marigolds, orange/yellow chrysanthemums. Growing US market
- **Thanksgiving (4th Thursday):** Centerpieces, hostess gifts. Pre-order campaign 3 weeks before
- **Holiday decor starts:** Wreaths, garlands, poinsettias begin selling
- **Revenue potential:** High

### December: Holiday Season
- **Christmas/Hanukkah:** Wreaths, centerpieces, poinsettias, corporate event florals
- **Corporate events/galas:** December is peak corporate event season
- **New Year's Eve:** Party arrangements, champagne-themed florals
- **Revenue potential:** Very High

# Flower Shop Social Media Calendar: What to Post Every Week in 2026

Most flower shops know they should be on social media. The problem isn't motivation — it's knowing what to post, when to post it, and making it sustainable when you're busy designing and delivering flowers.

This calendar gives you a repeatable weekly framework plus monthly themes so you never stare at a blank screen again.

## The Weekly Framework

Post 4-5 times per week using this rotating schedule:

| Day | Content Type | Example | Platform Priority |
|-----|-------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Monday** | Behind the scenes | Processing day, unboxing shipment, cooler tour | Instagram Reels, TikTok |
| **Tuesday** | Product feature | Single variety spotlight: name, care tips, fun fact | Instagram feed, Pinterest |
| **Wednesday** | Education/tips | "How to make your flowers last longer" or design technique | Instagram carousel, blog link |
| **Thursday** | Client showcase | Delivery photo, client testimonial, wedding feature | Instagram Stories, Facebook |
| **Friday** | Weekend ready | New arrangements available, weekend specials, "order now" CTA | Instagram feed, Facebook |

### Why This Framework Works

- **Consistency:** Same rhythm every week = easier to create, easier for followers to expect
- **Variety:** Covers every content pillar (BTS, product, education, social proof, sales)
- **Sustainable:** 4-5 posts/week is achievable for a busy shop. 7/day is not
- **Platform-appropriate:** Each post type naturally fits the platform where it performs best

## Monthly Content Themes

### January: Fresh Start
- New year arrangements, winter whites, "new year, new flowers" messaging
- Behind the scenes: your flower goals for the year
- **Key dates:** New Year's (1st), MLK Day (20th)
- **Hashtags:** #NewYearFlowers #FreshStart #WinterBlooms

### February: Love Season
- Valentine's Day countdown (start Feb 1)
- Pre-order reminders, gift guides, pricing tiers
- Behind the scenes: Valentine's prep chaos (people LOVE this content)
- **Key dates:** Valentine's Day (14th)
- **Hashtags:** #ValentinesFlowers #ValentinesDay #SendFlowers

### March: Spring Awakening
- Spring flower arrivals, tulips, daffodils, ranunculus
- Color explosion — bright, fresh palettes
- **Key dates:** Int'l Women's Day (8th), St. Patrick's (17th), Spring Equinox (20th)
- **Hashtags:** #SpringFlowers #SpringBlooms #FirstDayOfSpring

### April: Easter + Events
- Easter arrangements, pastel palettes
- Admin Professionals Day promotions (corporate orders)
- Spring wedding season begins — showcase bridal work
- **Key dates:** Easter (varies), Admin Professionals Day (last Wed)
- **Hashtags:** #EasterFlowers #SpringWedding #AdminProfessionalsDay

### May: Mother's Day Takeover
- Mother's Day countdown (start May 1)
- Pre-order campaigns, gift guides by budget
- Behind the scenes: Mother's Day week frenzy
- Post-Mother's Day: thank your team, show the aftermath
- **Key dates:** Mother's Day (2nd Sunday), Memorial Day (last Mon)

### June: Wedding + Summer
- Wedding season showcase — bouquets, centerpieces, arches

- **Key dates:** Father's Day (3rd Sunday), graduation season

### July: Summer Vibes
- Tropical arrangements, bright colors, outdoor events

## Platform Strategy

### Instagram (Primary)
- **Feed:** 3-4 posts/week (product, education, client features)
- **Stories:** Daily (quick BTS, polls, questions, reposts)
- **Reels:** 2-3/week (BTS videos, design process, unboxing)
- **Best times:** Tuesday-Friday, 10 AM-12 PM and 6-8 PM local time

### TikTok (Growth)
- **Focus:** BTS and process videos. "Watch me make this bouquet" performs
- **Frequency:** 3-5/week (repurpose Reels content)
- **Best content:** Fast-paced arrangement builds, flower unboxing, "day in the life"
- **Tip:** Sound matters. Use trending sounds

### Facebook (Existing Clients)
- **Focus:** Promotions, event posts, community engagement
- **Frequency:** 3-4/week (can cross-post from Instagram)
- **Best for:** Local reach, event promotion, older demographics, Facebook Marketplace
- **Groups:** Join and engage in local community groups and florist groups

### Pinterest (SEO)
- **Focus:** Portfolio of finished arrangements, organized by occasion and style
- **Frequency:** 5-10 pins/week (can bulk-schedule)
- **Best for:** Wedding traffic, long-term discovery, driving website visits
- **Tip:** Every pin should link to your website or shop page

## Content Creation Efficiency

### Batch Create Content

Don't create content daily. Batch it:

| When | What |
|------|------|
| **Monday morning (30 min)** | Take 5-10 product photos during processing |
| **One evening/week (1 hour)** | Write captions for the whole week |
| **Saturday (15 min)** | Shoot 1-2 Reels/TikToks during busy shop time |
| **Monthly (2 hours)** | Plan next month's theme, schedule posts, review analytics |

**Total time:** 3-4 hours/week. That's it.

### Tools That Help

## Measuring Success

| Metric | Why | Target |
|--------|-----|--------|
| **Follower growth** | Audience building | +50-100/month (small shop), +200-500 (established) |

| **DM inquiries** | Direct leads | Track and respond within 1 hour |
| **Orders from social** | Revenue attribution | Ask every caller/orderer "How did you find us?" |

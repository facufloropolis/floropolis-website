# Wholesale Wedding Flowers: The Florist's Complete Sourcing & Pricing Guide (2026)

Weddings are where florists make real money. The markup is higher (3-5x vs 2-3x retail), the order sizes are bigger, and a single wedding can generate $2,000-$15,000+ in revenue.

But wedding flowers are also where sourcing mistakes hurt the most. Wrong color, short stems, wilted blooms on the big day — and you lose a client forever plus every referral they would have sent.

This guide covers how to source, price, and plan wholesale flowers for weddings — from the most popular varieties to the markup formulas that keep you profitable.

## Why Wedding Flowers Are Your Highest-Margin Category

| Category | Typical Markup | Avg. Order Value | Why |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|-----|
| Daily retail | 2.5-3x | $35-75 | Walk-in, price-sensitive |
| Subscriptions | 2-2.5x | $25-50/week | Volume, recurring |
| **Weddings** | **3-5x** | **$2,000-15,000+** | Premium, emotional, one-time |
| Corporate | 2.5-3.5x | $200-1,000/month | Recurring, but price-negotiated |

A $5,000 wedding with a 4x markup means ~$3,750 gross profit from one event. Compare that to selling 100 daily arrangements at $45 each for the same revenue.

**The bottom line:** If you're not doing weddings, you're leaving the most profitable segment of floristry on the table.

## Wedding Flower Pricing: The Formula

### The Basic Formula

**Wedding arrangement price = (Flower cost × 3-5) + Hard goods + Labor + Delivery**

| Component | How to Calculate | Example |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| Flower cost | Sum of all stems at wholesale | $45 |
| Markup | × 3.5 (standard wedding) | $157.50 |
| Hard goods | Vase, foam, ribbon, pins | $15 |
| Labor | $25-50/hour × hours worked | $37.50 (1.5 hrs) |
| Delivery | $50-150 per event | Split across all items |
| **Arrangement price** | | **$210** |

### The Pricing Mistake Most Florists Make

New florists price weddings like retail: stem cost × 2.5. **This is wrong for weddings.**

Wedding work includes:
- **Consultation time** (1-3 hours per wedding, unpaid at 2.5x markup)
- **Sourcing** (custom varieties, color matching, trial arrangements)
- **Prep day** (4-8 hours for a typical wedding)
- **Setup day** (2-6 hours on-site)
- **Teardown** (1-3 hours if you offer it)
- **Transport** (vehicle, gas, wear and tear)

At 2.5x markup, you're often working below minimum wage when you account for all these hours. At 3.5-5x markup, you're properly compensated and profitable.

## How to Source Wedding Flowers

### The Ordering Timeline

| When | What to Do |
|------|-----------|
| **8-12 months before** | Client consultation, mood board, preliminary variety list |
| **3-4 months before** | Confirm varieties, check seasonal availability |
| **6 weeks before** | Place wholesale order for any farm-direct stems |
| **4 weeks before** | Confirm order, check for substitutions |
| **2 weeks before** | Order greens and fillers |
| **1 week before** | Confirm all deliveries, prepare backup plan |
| **3-4 days before** | Receive farm-direct flowers, begin processing |
| **1-2 days before** | Prep all arrangements, load vehicle |
| **Day of** | Setup, touch-ups, teardown |

### Sourcing Strategy

**Primary source (70-80%): Farm-direct wholesale**
- Best prices, freshest stems (12-14 day vase life)
- 4-day delivery from Ecuador/Colombia
- Order 6 weeks ahead for guaranteed allocation
- Floropolis: 270+ varieties, prices from $0.65/stem

**Secondary source (15-20%): Local wholesale market**
- Same-day availability for emergencies
- Higher prices but instant access
- Use for: last-minute additions, client changes, "oops I need 10 more stems"

**Specialty source (5-10%): Local growers**
- Unique varieties you can't get wholesale
- Seasonal specials (garden roses, dahlias, wildflowers)
- Great story for clients: "These were grown 20 miles from your venue"

### The Emergency Plan

Wedding flowers MUST arrive on time. Always have:
1. **Backup varieties** pre-identified (if Vendela is late, Playa Blanca substitutes)
2. **Local wholesale market** scouted and hours memorized
3. **Extra stems ordered** (10-15% buffer on all wedding orders)
4. **Another florist's number** for true emergencies (build relationships, not walls)

## Building Your Wedding Flower Business

### Step 1: Build a Portfolio
You need 10+ styled arrangements photographed professionally (or with great iPhone skills). If you have zero weddings, offer to do 2-3 at cost in exchange for photos.

### Step 2: Partner With Venues
The fastest path to wedding clients is venue referrals. Visit 3-5 local venues, bring a sample arrangement, and offer a referral commission (10% of flower cost or $100-200 flat).

### Step 3: Show Up Where Brides Search
- **Instagram:** Post every wedding. Use location tags. Use #[cityname]weddingflorist
- **Google Business Profile:** Optimize for "wedding florist [city]"
- **The Knot / WeddingWire:** Free listing, paid for premium placement
- **Bridal shows:** 1-2 per year max (time-intensive but pipeline-filling)

### Step 4: Price Confidently
Brides expect to spend $1,500-5,000 on wedding flowers. Don't apologize for your prices. Show the value (consultations, custom sourcing, day-of setup, premium farm-direct quality).

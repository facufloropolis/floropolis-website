# How to Start a Flower Delivery Business: Complete Guide for 2026

A flower delivery business is one of the leanest ways to enter the floral industry. No storefront. No walk-in cooler. No foot-traffic dependence. Just flowers, a vehicle, and a delivery area.

Whether you're starting from scratch or adding delivery to an existing flower business, this guide covers the practical steps: costs, logistics, pricing, sourcing, and how to get your first clients.

## Why Flower Delivery (vs. a Traditional Flower Shop)

| Factor | Traditional Shop | Delivery-Only Business |
|--------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Startup cost | $15,000-60,000 | $5,000-20,000 |
| Monthly rent | $1,500-5,000 | $0 (home-based) |
| Staff needed | 1-3 from day one | Just you to start |
| Revenue dependency | Walk-in + delivery | Delivery + online only |
| Profit margin | 8-15% net (high overhead) | 15-25% net (low overhead) |
| Scalability | Limited by location | Limited by delivery area |

**The delivery-only model works because:** Customers increasingly order flowers online and expect delivery. A delivery business captures the same demand as a shop without the rent, utilities, and staffing costs that eat into margins.

## The Delivery-Only Business Model

### How It Works

1. Client orders via your website, Instagram DM, phone, or text
2. You source flowers wholesale (farm-direct for planned orders, local market for same-day)
3. You design the arrangement in your home studio
4. You deliver within your service area
5. Payment via Square, Stripe, or Venmo

### Revenue Streams

| Stream | Price Range | Volume Potential |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Individual bouquet delivery | $45-85 | High (daily orders) |
| Subscriptions (weekly/biweekly) | $35-55/delivery | Recurring (monthly) |
| Event/wedding flowers | $500-5,000+ per event | Seasonal |
| Corporate weekly arrangements | $50-150/delivery | Recurring |
| Holiday specials (V-Day, Mother's Day) | $55-120 | Seasonal spikes |
| Gift delivery (birthdays, anniversaries) | $50-100 | Steady |

### Service Area

**Start with a 10-15 mile radius from your home.** This keeps delivery efficient (30-45 minutes per delivery including drive time).

Expand only when demand justifies it. A 30-mile radius doubles your potential clients but quadruples your drive time and gas costs.

## Sourcing Strategy

### Weekly Standing Order (Farm-Direct)

For a delivery business doing 15-25 arrangements per week:

| Flower | Weekly Qty | Wholesale Cost |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Roses (mixed colors) | 60 stems | $55 |
| Eucalyptus | 30 stems | $19.50 |
| Seasonal focal (ranunculus/[delphinium](/wholesale-delphiniums)) | 30 stems | $28-52 |
| Filler (baby's breath/ruscus) | 25 stems | $11-15 |
| **Weekly total** | **145 stems** | **$113-141** |

This makes approximately 15-20 arrangements at $7-9 in flower cost each. At $55 retail average, that's $825-1,100/week in revenue from ~$115 in wholesale.

### Same-Day Orders

Keep a small buffer of versatile stems (roses + eucalyptus) in your fridge for same-day requests. For anything beyond that, charge a premium for same-day delivery ($10-15 surcharge) to cover the local wholesale market run.

### Why Farm-Direct Matters for Delivery

Farm-direct flowers arrive with 12-14 days of vase life. This means:
- Flowers look fresh when they arrive at the client's door
- You can prep arrangements 1-2 days before delivery without quality loss
- Less waste = more profit from each wholesale order
- Clients get longer enjoyment (leading to reviews and repeat orders)

## Pricing Your Deliveries

### The Formula

**Price = (Flower cost × 2.5-3.0) + Hard goods + Delivery fee**

### Example Price Menu

| Tier | Stems | Flower Cost | Hard Goods | Delivery | Total Price | Margin |
|------|-------|-------------|-----------|----------|-------------|--------|
| **Petite** | 8-10 | $6-8 | $3 | $12 | $45 | 53-58% |
| **Classic** | 12-15 | $10-13 | $4 | $12 | $65 | 55-60% |
| **Premium** | 18-22 | $15-20 | $5 | $12 | $85 | 56-61% |
| **Luxury** | 25-30 | $22-30 | $8 | $15 | $120 | 56-58% |

### Delivery Fee Strategy

| Approach | Delivery Fee | Minimum Order | Best For |
|----------|-------------|---------------|---------|
| Flat fee | $10-15 per delivery | $40 | Simplicity |
| Free delivery over $X | $0 over $65, $12 under | $40 | Encourages larger orders |
| Distance-based | $8-20 based on miles | $40 | Fair, covers real costs |
| Included in price | $0 (built into arrangement price) | $55 | Perceived as "free delivery" |

**Recommendation:** "Free delivery on orders over $65" encourages upsells and feels generous. Build the delivery cost into your arrangement pricing.

## Delivery Logistics

### Vehicle Requirements

Your personal car works fine to start. Requirements:
- Clean interior (flower-safe, no pet hair or debris)
- Flat cargo area or trunk space for boxes
- Air conditioning (essential in summer)
- Non-slip mat or crate to prevent arrangements from tipping

### Delivery Windows

| Window | Best For | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Morning (8-11 AM) | Corporate, office deliveries | Arrive before the workday |
| Midday (11 AM-2 PM) | Residential, surprise deliveries | Client likely home |
| Afternoon (2-5 PM) | Residential, after-school | Wider availability |
| Evening (5-7 PM) | Date night, special occasions | Premium surcharge possible |

### Batch Deliveries

Don't deliver one arrangement at a time. Batch 3-5 deliveries per route:

1. Plan your route the night before (Google Maps, Circuit app)
2. Group deliveries by area
3. Load in reverse delivery order (last delivery loaded first)
4. Complete all deliveries in one 2-3 hour window

**The efficiency math:** 5 individual trips × 45 minutes = 3.75 hours. 5 batched deliveries on one route = 2 hours. Save 1.75 hours per batch.

## Getting Your First 50 Clients

### Phase 1: First 10 Clients (Week 1-4)

1. **Personal network.** Tell everyone: friends, family, neighbors, social media. "I'm delivering fresh flowers in [area]. Who wants a bouquet this week?"
2. **Instagram launch.** Post your best 9 arrangements. Bio: "Fresh flower delivery in [City]. Order: [link]"
3. **Google Business Profile.** Set up immediately. This is how people find "flower delivery near me."
4. **Nextdoor post.** "Hi neighbors! I just launched a flower delivery service in [neighborhood]. This week only: $10 off your first order."
5. **Offer to 3 friends for free.** In exchange for honest photos and a Google review.

### Phase 2: 10-25 Clients (Month 2-3)

6. **Subscription launch.** Offer weekly delivery at $40/week. Get 5 subscribers = $200/week guaranteed.
7. **Coffee shop display.** Place a fresh arrangement at a local coffee shop with your card. Refresh weekly.
8. **Corporate outreach.** Email 10 local offices: "Free trial arrangement for your lobby this week."
9. **Mother's Day / holiday push.** Holiday demand is your biggest acquisition moment. Run Instagram ads ($5/day) 2 weeks before.

### Phase 3: 25-50 Clients (Month 4-6)

10. **Referral program.** "Refer a friend, get $10 off your next order."
11. **Wedding/event outreach.** Contact 5 local venues about being their recommended delivery florist.
12. **Google Ads** ($5-10/day) targeting "flower delivery [city]."
13. **Partner with a real estate agent.** Closing gift arrangements = recurring orders.

## Scaling Beyond 50 Clients

| Milestone | What Changes |
|-----------|-------------|
| **50 clients** | Hire part-time driver for deliveries. You focus on design |
| **100 clients** | Need dedicated cooler space (walk-in or large reach-in). Part-time designer |
| **200+ clients** | Consider a studio space (not a storefront — just production). Full-time driver + designer |
| **500+ clients** | Multiple delivery routes. Subscription management software. You're now managing a team |

## Common Mistakes

1. **Underpricing delivery.** Your time driving IS your business. If you spend 45 minutes on a $45 delivery with $15 in materials, you're making $30/hour minus gas. That's fine — but don't drop to $35 deliveries where you're making $15/hour.

2. **Too large a delivery area.** Start tight (10-15 miles). Expand when demand proves it. A 30-mile delivery at $45 is unprofitable.

3. **No same-day policy.** Decide upfront: do you offer same-day? If yes, charge a $10-15 surcharge. If no, set clear order deadlines.

4. **Ignoring seasons.** Valentine's Day and Mother's Day are your 10x revenue days. Start planning 6 weeks early. Stock up. Run ads. Don't miss them.

5. **No website.** Instagram alone isn't enough. You need a website where clients can see your menu, place orders, and pay. Squarespace or Shopify, $30-50/month.

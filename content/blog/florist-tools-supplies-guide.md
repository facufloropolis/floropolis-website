# Essential Florist Tools and Supplies: The Complete Startup Checklist

Whether you're opening a flower shop, starting a home-based floral business, or setting up a wedding florist studio, you need the right tools. This guide covers every essential tool and supply, organized by priority — what to buy first, what can wait, and what the pros actually use.

## The Absolute Essentials (Buy These First)

### Cutting Tools

| Tool | Price Range | Recommendation | Why |
|------|-----------|---------------|-----|
| **Floral shears** | $15-40 | Sakagen or ARS | The single most important tool. Sharp, clean cuts = better water uptake |
| **Floral knife** | $10-25 | Victorinox | For woody stems, stripping bark, detail work |
| **Wire cutters** | $8-15 | Any brand | For cutting floral wire, chicken wire |
| **Stem stripper** | $5-10 | Rose stripper tool | Removes thorns and leaves in one motion |

**How to choose shears:** Spend $25+ on your primary shears. Cheap shears crush stems instead of cutting them, which blocks water uptake. Sakagen (Japanese-made) and ARS are the industry standards. Replace when they start crushing instead of cutting.

| Supply | Price | Use | Notes |

| **Floral tape (green)** | $2-4/roll | Wrapping wired stems, boutonnieres, corsages | Stretch as you wrap to activate adhesive |

| **Bind wire (paddle wire)** | $3-5/spool | Binding garlands, wreaths, bouquets | 24 gauge is the workhorse |

## Tool Maintenance

### Daily
- Wipe down shears with rubbing alcohol (prevents bacteria transfer between stems)
- Rinse buckets before refilling
- Clean work surfaces

### Weekly
- Sharpen shears (or send out for sharpening monthly)
- Deep clean buckets with bleach solution (1 tbsp per gallon)
- Inventory supplies — reorder before you run out

### Monthly
- Check cooler temperature calibration
- Replace any dull or damaged tools
- Clean cooler coils (dust reduces efficiency)

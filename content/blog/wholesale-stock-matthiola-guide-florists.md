# Wholesale Stock (Matthiola) for Florists: Varieties, Care, and Design Guide

Stock (Matthiola incana) is one of the most underappreciated workhorses in floral design. It provides height, fragrance, texture, and volume at a price point that makes it one of the highest-margin flowers in your cooler. At $1-3 per stem wholesale, a single stock stem fills the space of 3-4 filler flowers — making your arrangements look lush while keeping costs down.

This guide covers how to source, care for, and profit from stock flowers year-round.

## Care and Handling

Stock is relatively easy to care for, but has specific needs:

### At Receiving
1. **Unbox immediately** — stock is ethylene sensitive
2. **Remove lower leaves** aggressively — leaves below the waterline rot quickly and create bacteria
3. **Cut stems at 45 degrees** — stock stems are woody and need a fresh cut
4. **Place in cool water** with floral preservative
5. **Store at 36-40°F** — standard cooler temp is fine

### Key Care Points

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Water** | Heavy drinker. Check water level daily — stock depletes water fast |
| **Bacteria** | Leaves rot quickly in water. Strip aggressively. Change water if cloudy |
| **Ethylene** | Very sensitive. Keep away from fruit, exhaust, decaying flowers |
| **Fragrance** | Strong scent can be overpowering in enclosed spaces. Note for sympathy/hospital |
| **Stem strength** | Thick, woody stems. Can support themselves in arrangements. Good for foam-free design |
| **Bloom progression** | Bottom flowers open first, then progress upward. Remove spent lower blooms to extend life |

### Common Problems

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Lower blooms dropping | Normal aging (bottom-up bloom cycle) | Remove spent blooms, trim stem |
| Cloudy water/smell | Leaf matter rotting | Strip all foliage below waterline, change water |
| Wilting despite water | Bacterial blockage in stem | Fresh cut, clean water, flower food |
| Buds not opening | Harvested too tight, cold storage too long | Warm water, warm room. Some buds won't open if too tight |
| Yellowing leaves | Age or ethylene exposure | Remove affected leaves, check ethylene sources |

## Design Applications

### Wedding
- **Height element:** Stock in tall cylinder vases = dramatic, affordable centerpiece
- **Bridal bouquet:** 3-5 stock stems in a hand-tied bouquet add fragrance and fullness
- **Aisle markers:** Single stock stem per pew marker = elegant, fragrant, budget-friendly
- **Color matching:** Stock's wide color range means you can almost always match the wedding palette

### Everyday
- **Mixed arrangements:** 2-3 stock stems as the backbone, add 3-5 focal flowers. Arrangement looks $80, costs $25 to make
- **"Fragrance bouquet" promotion:** Stock + garden roses + sweet peas = the fragrance-focused offering
- **Grab-and-go wraps:** 5 stock stems, single color, paper-wrapped. $8 cost, $25-35 retail

### Sympathy
- **Casket sprays:** Stock provides bulk and height. Essential for large sympathy pieces
- **Standing sprays:** Stock + gladiolus for height, roses + carnations for focal weight
- **Note:** Stock's strong fragrance can be polarizing in viewing rooms. Mix with less-fragrant flowers

### Corporate
- **Lobby arrangements:** Stock provides impressive scale at modest cost. Weekly arrangements with stock as the backbone
- **Event buffet decor:** Tall stock arrangements add drama to long tables without blocking sightlines

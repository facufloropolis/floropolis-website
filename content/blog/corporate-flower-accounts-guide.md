# How to Land Corporate Flower Accounts: The Florist's B2B Sales Guide

Corporate flower accounts are the most underrated revenue stream in floristry. A single hotel lobby contract can be worth $500-2,000 per month. A restaurant group with 5 locations? $1,000-3,000/month. An office building with weekly arrangements? $200-600/month.

And unlike wedding or event work, corporate accounts are recurring. Month after month. No seasonal dips. No client acquisition cost after the first sale.

This guide covers how to find, pitch, win, and retain corporate flower clients.

## Why Corporate Accounts Are Worth Pursuing

| Factor | Weddings/Events | Corporate Accounts |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| Revenue per client | $2,000-10,000 (one-time) | $200-2,000/month (recurring) |
| Sales cycle | 3-12 months | 1-4 weeks |
| Client lifetime | One event | 12-36+ months |
| Annual value | One payment | $2,400-24,000/year |
| Predictability | Seasonal | Year-round |
| Design complexity | High (custom every time) | Low (same style weekly) |
| Delivery schedule | One-time | Fixed weekly route |

**A florist with 10 corporate accounts at $400/month average = $48,000/year in predictable recurring revenue.** That's a baseline that covers your operating costs before you sell a single retail bouquet or book a wedding.

## Types of Corporate Clients

### Hotels & Resorts
- **What they need:** Lobby arrangements, front desk displays, restaurant table flowers, event flowers
- **Budget:** $500-5,000/month per property
- **Delivery:** 1-3x/week
- **Decision maker:** Director of Operations, General Manager, or Purchasing Manager

### Restaurants & Bars
- **What they need:** Table centerpieces, bar arrangements, entrance displays
- **Budget:** $150-800/month per location
- **Delivery:** 1-2x/week
- **Decision maker:** Owner, General Manager, or Interior Designer

### Office Buildings & Co-Working Spaces
- **What they need:** Reception/lobby arrangement, conference room flowers, common area displays
- **Budget:** $200-600/month
- **Delivery:** 1x/week
- **Decision maker:** Office Manager, Facilities Manager, or Executive Assistant

### Real Estate (Staging & Open Houses)
- **What they need:** Staging arrangements for listings, open house flowers
- **Budget:** $50-200 per staging
- **Delivery:** As-needed (1-4x/month per agent)
- **Decision maker:** Real Estate Agent or Staging Company

### Medical Offices & Spas
- **What they need:** Reception arrangement, waiting room displays
- **Budget:** $150-400/month
- **Delivery:** 1x/week
- **Decision maker:** Office Manager or Owner

### Retail Stores & Boutiques
- **What they need:** Counter display, window arrangement
- **Budget:** $100-300/month
- **Delivery:** 1x/week
- **Decision maker:** Store Owner or Visual Merchandiser

## How to Price Corporate Accounts

### The Formula

**Weekly arrangement price = (Flower cost × 2.5-3.0) + Container + Delivery**

Corporate pricing is slightly lower per-arrangement than retail because:
- Guaranteed recurring revenue (no acquisition cost after initial sale)
- Simplified design (same style weekly, efficient production)
- Batch delivery (multiple accounts on one route)

### Pricing Guide

| Arrangement Size | Flower Cost | Container | Delivery | Weekly Price | Monthly (4x) |
|-----------------|-------------|-----------|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Small (reception desk) | $8-12 | Included | Included | $35-45 | $140-180 |
| Medium (lobby) | $15-25 | Included | Included | $55-85 | $220-340 |
| Large (hotel lobby) | $30-50 | Included | Included | $100-165 | $400-660 |
| Multiple locations | Varies | Included | Included | Custom quote | Custom |

**Container strategy:** Provide the vase/container and include it in your pricing. Swap it each week (bring the new arrangement in the same style container, take the old one). This makes the weekly swap seamless and the client never has to think about vases.

### Volume Discounts

| Monthly Spend | Discount |
|--------------|---------|
| $200-500/month | Standard pricing |
| $500-1,000/month | 5-10% discount |
| $1,000+/month | 10-15% discount + priority service |

## How to Find Corporate Clients

### The 20-Prospect List

Make a list of 20 businesses in your area that currently have flower arrangements (or should). Walk by their lobby. Look at their Instagram. Check if they have flowers.

**Where to look:**
1. Hotels within 15 miles of your studio
2. Upscale restaurants you've eaten at
3. Office buildings with visible lobbies
4. Real estate agents who sell luxury properties
5. Day spas and medical spas
6. Boutique retail stores
7. Wedding venues (they need weekly lobby flowers too)
8. Co-working spaces

### The Approach

**Step 1: Identify the decision maker.** Call the front desk: "Hi, I'm a local florist. Who handles your floral arrangements? I'd love to introduce myself."

**Step 2: Show up with a sample.** Don't email first. Walk in with a beautiful arrangement and say: "This is a complimentary arrangement for your lobby. I'd love to provide these weekly. Can I leave my card?"

**Step 3: Follow up in 3 days.** "How did your team like the arrangement? I'd love to set up a weekly service. Can we schedule 10 minutes to discuss?"

**Step 4: Present a simple proposal.** One page: what you'll deliver, how often, the price, and a photo of the sample arrangement.

## Retention: Keeping Corporate Clients

The average corporate flower account lasts 18-36 months if managed well. Here's how to keep them:

### 1. Be Reliable Above All Else
Deliver on the same day, same time, every week. Corporate clients value consistency over creativity. Being 100% reliable is more important than being 100% stunning.

### 2. Rotate Seasonally
Change the arrangement style with the seasons:
- Spring: Ranunculus, tulips, pastel roses
- Summer: Tropicals, bright colors, sunflowers
- Fall: Warm-toned roses, autumn greens, chrysanthemums
- Winter: White/green elegance, holiday accents in December

This keeps the service feeling fresh without the client having to ask.

### 3. Check In Monthly
A quick text or email: "How are you liking this month's arrangements? Any feedback or preferences?"

### 4. Holiday Upgrades
Upgrade the arrangement size at no charge during the holidays (Thanksgiving week, Christmas week). This costs you $10-20 extra in flowers but makes the client feel valued.

### 5. Annual Review
Once a year, schedule a brief meeting: "Here's a recap of the past year. Any changes to your space? Any events coming up? How can I improve?"

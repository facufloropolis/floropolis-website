## Care and Handling

### At Receiving
1. **Unpack immediately** — snapdragons are gravitropic (stems curve upward if laid flat)
2. **Store UPRIGHT from the moment you receive them** — this is critical
3. **Cut stems at 45 degrees**, remove lower foliage
4. **Place in cool water** with floral preservative
5. **Store at 36-40°F** — standard cooler temp is ideal
6. **Remove lowest florets** that are spent or damaged

### Key Care Points

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Gravitropism** | Snapdragons ALWAYS bend upward. If stored horizontally even briefly, stems will curve permanently. Always store and transport upright |
| **Bloom progression** | Bottom florets open first, then move upward. Remove spent lower blooms to extend life and improve appearance |
| **Water** | Moderate drinkers. Check every 2 days |
| **Temperature** | Prefer cool conditions. Heat accelerates bloom opening and reduces vase life |
| **Ethylene** | Sensitive. Keep away from fruit and exhaust |
| **Tip pinch** | Some florists remove the top 1-2 inches of unopened buds for a cleaner look in formal arrangements |

### Common Problems

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Curved/bent stems | Gravitropism from horizontal storage | Cannot be fully corrected. Prevent by ALWAYS storing upright |
| Florets dropping | Ethylene exposure or age | Remove from ethylene sources, accept as normal aging |
| Limp stems | Dehydration | Fresh cut + deep warm water for 2-4 hours |
| Only bottom florets opening | Normal. They open bottom-to-top | This is how snapdragons work. Remove spent lower blooms |
| Short vase life in summer | Heat stress | Source from cooler growing regions, keep cold |

## Design Applications

### Wedding
- **Ceremony arrangements:** Snapdragons provide the tall, garden-like flow that ceremony pieces need
- **Centerpiece accents:** 2-3 stems extending above the main arrangement create a garden-style silhouette
- **Bridal bouquet:** Butterfly snaps for a flowing, romantic bridal look
- **Aisle flowers:** Tall snaps in aisle urns for dramatic garden-church aesthetic

### Everyday
- **Mixed arrangements:** 2-3 snaps as the height element in any mixed bouquet
- **Grab-and-go:** 5 snapdragons + 5 roses = a complete, salable bouquet at low cost
- **"Garden fresh" collection:** Snaps + stock + delphinium + garden roses = English garden aesthetic

### Sympathy
- **Standing sprays:** Snapdragons provide essential height for vertical sympathy pieces
- **Casket sprays:** Trailing snaps along the edges add movement and softness

### Large-Scale/Events
- **Table runners:** Long, low arrangements with snaps providing the flowing lines
- **Altar pieces:** Snaps are the most cost-effective way to fill large ceremony arrangements
- **Buffet arrangements:** Tall snaps keep arrangements above buffet items without using expensive flowers

# Flower Wrapping & Packaging: Techniques, Materials, and Presentation Guide

Packaging is the first thing a customer sees when they receive flowers — and the last thing they remember. A beautifully wrapped bouquet feels like a $100 gift. The same flowers in a plastic sleeve feel like $20. Wrapping is where perceived value is created.

This guide covers wrapping techniques, materials, costs, and how to develop a signature packaging style for your shop.

## Wrapping Techniques

### 1. The Classic Cone Wrap

**Best for:** Single-variety bouquets, grab-and-go, market stalls

**Materials:** One sheet kraft or tissue paper + ribbon

**Method:**
1. Lay paper diamond-shaped on table
2. Place bouquet stems at bottom corner, blooms pointing to top corner
3. Fold left side over stems
4. Roll right side around, creating a cone shape
5. Secure with tape at the back
6. Tie with ribbon or twine at the gathering point

**Cost per wrap:** $0.25-0.75

### 2. The Korean-Style Wrap (Trending)

**Best for:** Premium bouquets, Instagram-worthy presentations, gift orders

**Materials:** 2-3 sheets Korean wrapping paper + ribbon or bow

**Method:**
1. Layer 2-3 sheets of different colored papers at slight angles
2. Place bouquet at center-bottom
3. Fold bottom up over stems
4. Bring sides in, creating soft pleats
5. Gather at gathering point, creating a "ruffle" effect at the top
6. Tie with satin ribbon
7. Fluff the paper edges for volume

**Cost per wrap:** $1.50-3.00
**Why it's popular:** The layered, textured wrap adds $10-20 in perceived value

### 3. The Hand-Tied European Wrap

**Best for:** European-style bouquets that stand on their own

**Materials:** Kraft paper or tissue + cellophane (for water)

**Method:**
1. Wrap stems in damp paper towel or small water bag
2. Cover stems with cellophane to contain moisture
3. Wrap in tissue paper (inner layer)
4. Wrap in kraft paper (outer layer)
5. Tie at gathering point
6. Bouquet should stand upright on flat stem base

**Cost per wrap:** $0.50-1.50

### 4. The Luxury Gift Wrap

**Best for:** Delivery orders, premium gifts, special occasions

**Materials:** Tissue paper + kraft or colored paper + cellophane + ribbon + care card

**Method:**
1. Inner: wrap stems in water source (tube or wet cotton)
2. Tissue paper layer around flowers (protection)
3. Outer paper wrap with clean edges
4. Cellophane outer sleeve (optional, for delivery protection)
5. Satin or grosgrain ribbon
6. Attach branded sticker and care card
7. Place in a sturdy carry bag for transport

**Cost per wrap:** $2-5
**Perceived value addition:** $15-25

### 5. The Minimal Modern Wrap

**Best for:** Contemporary shops, minimalist branding

**Materials:** Single sheet matte paper + single accent element

**Method:**
1. One clean sheet of paper (white, black, or neutral)
2. Wrap simply with clean folds
3. Single piece of ribbon, twine, or wax seal
4. Less is more — let the flowers be the focus

**Cost per wrap:** $0.30-1.00

## Building a Brand Through Packaging

### Your Signature Elements

Every shop should have 3-5 consistent brand elements in their packaging:

| Element | Cost to Implement | Impact |
|---------|------------------|--------|
| **Branded sticker** | $50-150 for 500-1,000 stickers | High (closes wrap, visible brand) |
| **Custom ribbon** | $100-300 for 100+ yards | High (recognizable from a distance) |
| **Branded care card** | $50-100 for 500 cards | Medium (adds professionalism + extends vase life) |
| **Signature paper color** | $0 (just choose and be consistent) | High (builds visual recognition) |
| **Branded bag** | $100-300 for 200+ bags | High (walking billboard) |

### The Unboxing Experience

For delivery orders, think about the customer's unboxing sequence:
1. **Outer bag/box** — branded, protective
2. **Tissue reveal** — open bag to see colored tissue
3. **Flowers visible** — arrangement or bouquet looking perfect
4. **Care card** — instructions + your brand + a personal touch
5. **Business card or next-order discount** — drives repeat purchase

Each step should feel intentional and premium.

## Sustainable Packaging Options

Eco-friendly packaging is increasingly expected, especially by younger clients:

| Sustainable Option | Replaces | Cost Comparison |
|-------------------|----------|-----------------|
| **Kraft paper** (recyclable) | Plastic wrapping | Similar cost |
| **Newspaper/book pages** | Virgin paper | Cheaper |
| **Raffia/twine** (compostable) | Plastic ribbon | Similar cost |
| **Water tubes** (reusable) | Plastic bags for water | Slightly more upfront, reusable |
| **Compostable cellophane** | Standard cellophane | 20-50% more expensive |
| **Reusable cloth wraps** | Paper wrapping | $1-3 per use, premium brand signal |

**Messaging opportunity:** "Our packaging is 100% recyclable/compostable" — this is a selling point worth advertising.

## Common Packaging Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| **Over-wrapping** | Let the flowers show. Wrapping should frame, not hide |
| **Cheap ribbon** | Curling ribbon looks amateur. Use satin, grosgrain, or natural fiber |
| **No water source for delivery** | Stems dry out during delivery. Always include water tube or wet cotton |
| **Inconsistent wrapping** | Every bouquet from your shop should look like it came from the same place |
| **No branding** | A sticker costs $0.10. Not having one is $0 in brand building |
| **Ignoring the stem area** | Exposed wet stems ruin the presentation. Wrap stems cleanly |

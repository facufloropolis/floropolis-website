# Wedding Flower Planning Timeline: Month-by-Month Checklist

Wedding flowers are one of the top 5 wedding expenses, yet most couples start planning them too late. A last-minute florist search means fewer choices, higher prices, and more stress.

This guide provides a complete month-by-month timeline for planning wedding flowers — whether you're a bride planning your own flowers, or a florist helping your clients stay on track.

## The Quick Timeline

| When | What to Do |
|------|-----------|
| **12-10 months before** | Research florists, book your florist, set flower budget |
| **8-6 months before** | Design consultation, choose color palette, confirm seasonal availability |
| **4-3 months before** | Finalize proposal, sign contract, pay deposit |
| **2-1 months before** | Final details meeting, confirm quantities, delivery logistics |
| **2-1 weeks before** | Final confirmation, last changes, emergency contact exchange |
| **Day of** | Delivery, setup, enjoy |

## 12-10 Months Before: Research and Book

### Set Your Flower Budget

The average American couple spends 8-10% of their total wedding budget on flowers.

| Total Budget | Flower Budget (8-10%) | What It Gets You |
|-------------|---------------------|-----------------|
| $15,000 | $1,200-1,500 | Personal flowers + simple centerpieces |
| $25,000 | $2,000-2,500 | Personal flowers + ceremony + centerpieces |
| $40,000 | $3,200-4,000 | Full floral design with installations |
| $60,000 | $4,800-6,000 | Luxury florals, multiple installations |
| $100,000+ | $8,000-10,000+ | Premium everything, custom design |

### Research Florists

**Where to find florists:**
- Instagram (search #[city]weddingflorist)
- The Knot / WeddingWire
- Venue recommendations
- Wedding planner referrals
- Google "[city] wedding florist"

**What to look for:**
- Style that matches your vision (look at their portfolio, not just their best photo)
- Experience with your venue (or similar venues)
- Within your budget range
- Good reviews and testimonials
- Responsive communication (if they take a week to reply now, imagine wedding week)

### Book Your Florist

Popular florists book 6-12 months in advance for peak wedding season (May-October). If your wedding is on a Saturday in June, book early.

**Questions to ask before booking:**
1. "Are you available on [date]?"
2. "What's your minimum budget for a wedding?"
3. "How many weddings do you do per weekend?" (More than 1 = potential quality/attention issues)
4. "Can I see examples of work at my venue or a similar one?"
5. "What's your consultation process?"
6. "What's included in your fee beyond flowers?" (Setup, teardown, containers, travel)

## 8-6 Months Before: Design Consultation

### The Consultation Meeting

This is where your florist translates your vision into a plan. Come prepared with:

- **Pinterest board** or mood board (20-30 images max — too many is confusing)
- **Color swatches** from bridesmaid dresses or décor
- **Venue photos** or a venue walkthrough
- **Budget range** (be honest — florists can work with any budget if they know what it is)
- **Must-haves** vs. nice-to-haves (bridal bouquet = must-have, aisle markers = nice-to-have)

### Choose Your Color Palette

Lock in your colors at this stage. Changing the palette at 2 months out means starting the design from scratch.

| Palette Type | Example | Best Season |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Classic | White + green | Any |
| Romantic | Blush + ivory + dusty rose | Spring, summer |
| Bold | Burgundy + navy + gold | Fall, winter |
| Bright | Coral + yellow + orange | Summer |
| Moody | Deep purple + burgundy + plum | Fall, winter |
| Earthy | Terracotta + sage + cream | Any |

### Confirm Seasonal Availability

Your florist should advise you on what's in season for your wedding month. In-season flowers are cheaper, fresher, and more beautiful.

| Month | In Season | Avoid (Expensive/Unavailable) |
|-------|----------|------------------------------|
| Jan-Feb | Anemones, ranunculus, tulips, amaryllis | Peonies, dahlias |
| Mar-Apr | Tulips, ranunculus, sweet peas, daffodils | Peonies (early), dahlias |
| May-Jun | Peonies, garden roses, ranunculus, sweet peas | Dahlias (early), amaryllis |
| Jul-Aug | Dahlias, sunflowers, zinnias, garden roses | Peonies (over), tulips |
| Sep-Oct | Dahlias, chrysanthemums, roses, marigolds | Peonies, tulips |
| Nov-Dec | Amaryllis, anemones, roses, evergreens | Peonies, dahlias, sunflowers |

**Pro tip:** Roses, eucalyptus, and hydrangeas are available year-round and work in virtually any palette.

## 4-3 Months Before: Finalize and Sign

### Review the Proposal

Your florist's proposal should include:

- [ ] Itemized list of all flower elements (bouquets, centerpieces, etc.)
- [ ] Specific flower varieties (or "florist's choice in [color]" for budget flexibility)
- [ ] Container/vessel descriptions
- [ ] Setup and teardown details
- [ ] Delivery logistics
- [ ] Substitution policy
- [ ] Total cost with payment schedule
- [ ] Cancellation/refund policy

### Budget Allocation Guide

How to divide your flower budget:

| Element | % of Flower Budget | Why |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| Personal flowers (bouquets, bouts, corsages) | 25-30% | These are in every photo |
| Ceremony décor | 15-20% | Sets the scene for vows |
| Reception centerpieces | 35-40% | Largest quantity, biggest visual impact |
| Additional (cake, cocktail, misc) | 10-15% | Finishing touches |

### Sign the Contract and Pay Deposit

Standard wedding florist payment terms:
- **Deposit:** 25-50% at contract signing (non-refundable)
- **Midpoint payment:** 25-40% at 30-60 days before wedding
- **Balance:** Remaining 10-25% at 7-14 days before wedding

## 2-1 Months Before: Final Details

### The Final Meeting

Schedule a 30-minute final meeting (in person or video) to confirm:

1. **Final guest count** → final centerpiece count
2. **Any design changes** (this is your last chance)
3. **Delivery timeline** — what time, where, who receives
4. **Setup details** — who handles ceremony to reception repurposing?
5. **Emergency contact** — your florist's cell phone for day-of issues
6. **Weather plan** — if outdoor ceremony moves inside, what changes?

## Day Of: What to Expect

### Delivery and Setup Timeline

| Time (before ceremony) | Activity |
|----------------------|---------|
| 4-5 hours | Florist arrives at venue |
| 3-4 hours | Ceremony setup begins |
| 2-3 hours | Reception centerpieces placed |
| 1-2 hours | Personal flowers delivered to bridal suite |
| 30 minutes | Final walkthrough and touch-ups |
| Ceremony | Everything should be set |

### Tips for the Couple on Wedding Day

- **Don't carry your bouquet by the stems.** Hold it at the binding point or have your MOH carry it when you're not in photos
- **Keep bouquets in water** between ceremony and reception photos
- **Designate someone** to move ceremony flowers to reception (if repurposing)
- **Plan for bouquet toss** — have your florist make a separate, smaller toss bouquet ($20-30) so you can keep your real one

# Wholesale Peonies: Season, Pricing, and Alternatives for Florists

Peonies are the most requested flower in wedding consultations. Brides love them. Instagram loves them. And florists either love them or dread them — because sourcing peonies reliably at a reasonable price is one of the biggest challenges in wholesale floristry.

This guide covers everything you need to know about sourcing wholesale peonies: when they're available, what they cost, how to care for them, and — crucially — what to offer when they're out of season.

## Peony Season: When Are They Available?

Peonies have a limited natural season. Unlike roses or eucalyptus, they cannot be grown year-round — even in Ecuador.

### Availability Calendar

| Month | Availability | Source | Pricing |
|-------|-------------|--------|---------|
| January | Not available | — | — |
| February | Not available | — | — |
| March | Rare (Southern Hemisphere imports) | Chile, New Zealand | Very premium ($6-10/stem) |
| April | Starting (limited) | Netherlands, domestic US | Premium ($4-7/stem) |
| **May** | **Peak season** | **Netherlands, domestic US** | **$3-5/stem wholesale** |
| **June** | **Peak season** | **Netherlands, domestic US, Alaska** | **$3-5/stem wholesale** |
| July | Ending (limited) | Alaska, late domestic | Premium ($4-7/stem) |
| August | Rare | Late Alaska blooms | Very premium |
| September-December | Not available | — | — |

**The reality:** True peony season is May and June. Outside this window, peonies are either unavailable, extremely expensive, or both.

### Why Peonies Are Expensive

1. **Short growing season** — Unlike roses (year-round in Ecuador), peonies bloom for 6-8 weeks
2. **Perishable** — 5-7 day vase life, shorter than most wholesale flowers
3. **Demand exceeds supply** — Every bride wants them, not enough farms grow them
4. **Import costs** — Off-season peonies ship from the Southern Hemisphere at premium freight rates
5. **Bloom stage matters** — Must be cut at exactly the right stage. Too early = they never open. Too late = they wilt in 2 days

## Peony Care: Maximizing Vase Life

Peonies are more temperamental than most wholesale flowers. Proper care is the difference between 3 days and 7 days of vase life.

### On Arrival

1. **Unpack immediately.** Peonies are ethylene-sensitive and heat-sensitive.
2. **Check bloom stage.** Stems should feel like a soft marshmallow when squeezed gently. Hard = too tight (may not open). Soft and loose = too far open (short life).
3. **Cut stems at a sharp angle.** Remove 2 inches.
4. **Remove ALL leaves below the water line.** Peony foliage rots quickly and contaminates water.
5. **Place in clean, cool water.** Room temperature water to start, then move to cooler.

### Storage

- **Temperature:** 34-36°F (1-2°C). Colder than most flowers. Peonies in a warm room will blow open in hours.
- **Keep away from ethylene sources.** No fruit, no rotting flowers, no gas sources nearby.
- **Store tight buds separately.** If you receive tight buds, they can hold for 2-3 days at 34°F. This gives you a longer selling window.

### Forcing Peonies to Open

Need them open for an event tomorrow?
1. Cut stems fresh
2. Place in warm water (100-110°F / 38-43°C)
3. Set in a warm room (70°F+)
4. Check every 2-3 hours
5. Once open to desired stage, move back to cool water and cooler to hold

### Holding Peonies Closed

Need to slow them down for a weekend event?
1. Keep at 34°F minimum
2. Don't cut stems until ready to use
3. Keep in low light
4. They'll hold 2-3 extra days in cold storage

## The Peony Alternatives: What to Offer When They're Out of Season

This is where most florists get stuck. A bride asks for peonies in September, and you have three options:
1. Say "they're not available" (loses the sale)
2. Source off-season imports at $8-10/stem (kills your margin)
3. **Offer a beautiful alternative that looks similar** (wins the sale AND the margin)

### Best Peony Alternatives

| Alternative | Looks Like | Wholesale | Season | Vase Life |
|------------|-----------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Garden Roses** | Very similar — ruffled, full bloom | $2.50-4.00/stem | May-October | 7-10 days |
| **Ranunculus (jumbo)** | Similar layered petals | $1.74/stem | Dec-May | 10-12 days |
| **Lisianthus** | Ruffled, rose-like | $1.50-2.50/stem | Year-round | 10-14 days |
| **Dahlia (dinner plate)** | Large, full bloom | Market price | Jul-Oct | 5-7 days |
| **Carnation (ruffled)** | Petal texture similar | $0.40-0.60/stem | Year-round | 14+ days |

### The Conversation Script

When a client asks for peonies out of season:

> "I love your taste — peonies are gorgeous. They're seasonal (May-June), so for your [month] wedding, I'd recommend [alternative]. It has the same lush, romantic look as peonies but it's in peak season, which means better quality and more reliable supply. Let me show you some examples..."

**Show them photos.** Most clients who want peonies want the *look* — full, ruffled, romantic blooms. Garden roses and jumbo ranunculus deliver that look year-round at better prices.

### The Peony + Alternative Mix

For peak season weddings (May-June), the best approach is mixing peonies with similar flowers:
- **3 peonies + 7 garden roses** per bridal bouquet (looks 100% peony, costs 40% less)
- **Peony focal flowers + ranunculus filler** in centerpieces
- **Save peonies for high-visibility pieces** (bridal bouquet, sweetheart table) and use alternatives for everything else

This gives the client the "peony wedding" they want while keeping your costs manageable.

## Ordering Peonies Wholesale

### Planning Timeline

| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| **January** | Discuss peony expectations with spring/summer brides |
| **March** | Pre-order from your wholesale source (allocations fill early) |
| **April** | Confirm order quantities |
| **May** | First shipments arrive — season begins |
| **June** | Peak availability — order freely |
| **July** | Season ending — last call |

### Sourcing Tips

1. **Pre-order early.** Peony supply is allocated, not unlimited. Farms sell out 4-6 weeks before peak season.
2. **Order tight buds.** Peonies cut tight last longer in transit and give you more control over when they open.
3. **Have a backup.** Always have a garden rose or ranunculus backup order ready in case your peony order is short.
4. **Don't over-order.** Peonies have the shortest vase life of any premium flower (5-7 days). Order for specific events, not for general stock.
5. **Check your substitution policy.** What happens if your supplier can't fulfill the full order? Know this in advance.

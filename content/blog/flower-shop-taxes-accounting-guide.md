# Flower Shop Taxes & Accounting: What Every Florist Needs to Know

Flower shop taxes have unique complexities that general small business guides don't cover: perishable inventory write-offs, sales tax on delivery charges, wire service commission accounting, and the seasonal income patterns that make quarterly estimates tricky.

This guide covers the tax and accounting essentials every florist needs — whether you're just starting or you've been filing incorrectly for years.

**Disclaimer:** This is educational guidance, not tax advice. Consult a CPA or tax professional for your specific situation.

## Business Structure: Which Entity Type?

| Structure | Best For | Tax Treatment | Liability Protection |
|-----------|---------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Sole Proprietorship** | Side hustle, flower stands | Income on personal return (Schedule C) | None |
| **LLC (Single-Member)** | Most small flower shops | Income on personal return (Schedule C) or elect S-Corp | Personal asset protection |
| **LLC (Multi-Member)** | Partnerships, co-owned shops | Partnership return (Form 1065) | Personal asset protection |
| **S-Corporation** | Shops making $60K+ net profit | Salary + distributions (saves self-employment tax) | Full corporate protection |
| **C-Corporation** | Large operations, seeking investors | Corporate tax (21%) + dividend tax | Full corporate protection |

**Most common path:** Start as LLC → elect S-Corp taxation when net profit exceeds $50-60K/year. This saves 7-15% in self-employment taxes on the distribution portion.

### The S-Corp Savings Example

| Scenario | LLC (Schedule C) | S-Corp Election |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Net profit | $100,000 | $100,000 |
| Reasonable salary | — | $50,000 |
| Distribution | — | $50,000 |
| Self-employment tax (15.3%) | $14,130 | $7,065 (on salary only) |
| **Annual savings** | — | **~$7,065** |

## Sales Tax for Flower Shops

Sales tax is the most common tax compliance issue for florists.

### What's Taxable?

| Item | Taxable? | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| **Flower arrangements** | Yes | Standard retail sale |
| **Loose stems/bunches** | Yes | Standard retail sale |
| **Plants** | Yes (usually) | Some states exempt food-producing plants |
| **Delivery charges** | Varies by state | ~25 states tax delivery, ~25 don't |
| **Wire service orders (outgoing)** | Varies | You may owe tax on the full order or just your commission |
| **Wire service orders (incoming/filling)** | Usually no | You're a subcontractor filling the order |
| **Gift cards** | No | Tax collected when card is redeemed |
| **Event/wedding services** | Varies | Some states tax labor separately from product |
| **Vases sold separately** | Yes | Standard retail |

### Delivery Charge Tax Rules (Key States)

| State | Delivery Taxable? |
|-------|-------------------|
| **California** | Yes, if not separately stated |
| **New York** | Yes |
| **Texas** | Yes |
| **Florida** | No (if separately stated on invoice) |
| **Illinois** | No |
| **Pennsylvania** | No |
| **Ohio** | Yes |

**Action item:** Check your specific state's rules. Many florists have been collecting (or not collecting) delivery tax incorrectly for years.

## Inventory Accounting for Perishables

This is where flower shop accounting gets unique.

### The Perishable Problem

Standard retail inventory accounting assumes inventory holds its value until sold. Flowers don't — they lose ALL value in 5-14 days if unsold.

### How to Account for Flower Waste

**Method 1: Track Actual Waste (Recommended)**
- Count unsold/discarded flowers weekly
- Record at cost value
- Include in COGS (reduces taxable income)
- Keep a simple waste log: date, item, quantity, cost

**Method 2: Estimated Waste Percentage**
- Industry average waste: 15-25% of flower purchases
- Apply estimated percentage to total flower purchases
- Less accurate but simpler for small shops

### The Waste Log

| Date | Item | Qty | Unit Cost | Total Write-Off |
|------|------|-----|-----------|----------------|
| 3/7 | Red roses (excess V-Day) | 50 stems | $1.25 | $62.50 |
| 3/7 | Mixed tulips (wilted) | 20 stems | $0.75 | $15.00 |
| 3/7 | Greenery (excess) | 5 bunches | $4.00 | $20.00 |
| **Weekly total** | | | | **$97.50** |

**At 20% waste on $5,000/month flower purchases, you're writing off $12,000/year in spoilage.** If you're not tracking this, you're overpaying taxes.

## Quarterly Estimated Taxes

Flower shop income is highly seasonal, which makes quarterly estimates challenging.

### The Seasonal Income Problem

| Quarter | Typical Revenue Share | Tax Estimate Challenge |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Q1 (Jan-Mar) | 20-30% (Valentine's) | Big February, low January = uneven |
| Q2 (Apr-Jun) | 30-35% (Mother's Day + weddings) | Highest revenue quarter |
| Q3 (Jul-Sep) | 15-20% | Lowest quarter, might overpay estimates |
| Q4 (Oct-Dec) | 20-30% (holidays) | December surge makes estimates tricky |

### How to Handle Quarterly Estimates

**Option 1: Annualized Income Method**
- Pay estimated taxes based on ACTUAL income each quarter (not 25% of last year's total)
- More accurate for seasonal businesses
- Requires more record-keeping but avoids overpaying in slow quarters

**Option 2: Safe Harbor (Prior Year)**
- Pay 100% of LAST year's tax liability, divided by 4
- Simple but may mean overpaying in slow quarters and underpaying in busy ones
- If you owed $12,000 last year, pay $3,000/quarter regardless of this year's income

**Recommendation:** Use the annualized method if your revenue varies more than 30% between quarters (most flower shops do).

## When to Hire a CPA

### DIY is Fine If:
- Revenue under $100K
- No employees (just you)
- Simple business structure (sole prop or single-member LLC)
- You're comfortable with QuickBooks or similar software

### Hire a CPA When:
- Revenue exceeds $100K
- You have employees (payroll tax compliance gets complex)
- You're considering S-Corp election
- You have wire service income (commission accounting)
- You do wedding/event work (contract accounting)
- You've been audited or received a tax notice

### What a CPA Costs

| Service | Annual Cost |
|---------|------------|
| Tax preparation only | $500-1,500 |
| Monthly bookkeeping + tax prep | $2,400-6,000 |
| Full-service (bookkeeping + payroll + tax + advisory) | $5,000-12,000 |

**The ROI:** A good CPA typically saves 2-5× their fee in identified deductions, better tax structure, and avoided penalties.
